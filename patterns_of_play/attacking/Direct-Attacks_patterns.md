# Attack Form 4: Direct Attacks (4-3-3)

Direct attacking is about **speed of progression with minimal buildup**, but unlike fast breaks (Form 3), it is usually **planned, structured, and repeatable**, not purely transitional chaos.

Typical objective: reach the final third in **1–4 passes**, then attack immediately.

I’ll break it into the main direct attack patterns and show what **each of the 11 players does moment-by-moment**.

---

# Pattern 1: Direct Ball to Striker (Classic Target Progression)

## Situation

Ball is in defensive third or deep midfield.

```text id="d1s9qa"
      ST
LW          RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
       GK
```

---

## Step 1: Build Trigger

### Center-Backs (CBs)

* Receive pressure or space opens.
* Immediately scan forward.

### Actions

* Skip midfield entirely.
* Look for striker or wide runner.

### Movement

* Step slightly forward to increase passing angle.

---

## Step 2: Long Pass Execution

### Ball-Side CB

```text id="8kq2mx"
LCB → ST
```

* Driven or lofted pass.
* Target is striker’s chest or feet.

### Far CB

* Drops deeper.
* Covers for second ball.

---

## Step 3: Striker Phase

### Striker (ST)

This is the key player.

### Movement options:

#### 1. Hold-up position

```text id="t3m8qv"
ST ↓ (backs to goal)
```

* Receives under pressure.
* Shields ball.

#### 2. Channel run

```text id="p6v2ld"
ST ↗
```

* Attacks space behind defense.

#### 3. Flick-on setup

* Jumps to redirect ball.

---

## Step 4: Support Runs

### Wingers (LW + RW)

They do NOT stay wide at first.

They sprint:

* LW → inside left channel
* RW → inside right channel

```text id="k8xq1z"
LW ↗   ST   RW ↖
```

Purpose:

* Win second ball
* Attack flick-on

---

### Midfielders (LCM / RCM)

### Actions

* Sprint beyond striker.
* Attack second phase.

### Movement

* LCM → left edge of box
* RCM → right edge of box

---

### Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Follows play.
* Wins second balls.
* Protects against counter.

---

## Step 5: Second Ball Phase

If striker wins duel:

### Option A: Layoff

```text id="z9w2fp"
ST → CM
```

CM shoots or passes wide.

---

### Option B: Flick-on

```text id="q4t7ln"
ST → RW
```

RW breaks behind defense.

---

### Option C: Turn

ST drives forward.

Shoots quickly.

---

## End Structure

```text id="a7v9kc"
      LW   ST   RW
        LCM RCM
           DM
```

All attackers arrive within 3–5 seconds.

---

# Pattern 2: Channel Direct Attack

Used when space exists behind fullbacks.

---

## Step 1: Trigger

CB or GK sees space behind defense.

---

## Step 2: Wide Target

### Fullback or CB plays:

```text id="c5r8pt"
CB → LW channel
```

or

```text id="h2k9ld"
CB → RW channel
```

---

## Step 3: Winger Movement

### LW / RW

* Sprint into channel (between CB and FB).

```text id="m8t3vq"
LW ↗
```

* Do NOT receive stationary.

---

## Step 4: Striker Movement

### ST

* Attacks near-post zone.

```text id="s4x6qn"
ST →
```

* Pulls center-backs inward.

---

## Step 5: Midfield Runs

### LCM / RCM

* Attack box late.
* Target cutback zone.

---

### DM

* Holds outside box for rebound.

---

## Final Shape

```text id="v7k1mz"
   ST

LW       RW

LCM   RCM
```

---

# Pattern 3: Second-Ball Direct Attack

Very common in physical or aerial teams.

---

## Step 1: Long Ball

### CB or GK

```text id="f3x8qp"
GK → midfield/striker zone
```

Target:

* Striker
* Wing area
* Central duel

---

## Step 2: Aerial Duel

### Striker

* Jumps or contests.

### CB opponent

* Challenges directly.

---

## Step 3: All Midfielders React

### LCM + RCM

* Sprint toward drop zone.

```text id="u2m9lk"
CM → second ball zone
```

---

### DM

* Covers deeper space.

---

### Wingers

* Move inside aggressively.

---

## Step 4: Loose Ball Phase

Three possibilities:

### 1. Win cleanly → immediate shot

### 2. Knockdown → midfielder shot

### 3. Defensive clearance → recycle attack

---

## Key Principle

Everyone attacks the **landing zone**, not the original ball.

---

# Pattern 4: Target-Man Layoff Attack

---

## Step 1: Direct Entry Pass

```text id="n4k8zd"
CB → ST
```

---

## Step 2: Striker Holds Ball

### ST actions:

* Receives under pressure.
* Uses body to shield.
* Waits for support.

---

## Step 3: Supporting Structure Forms

### Midfielders

* LCM → left shooting lane
* RCM → right shooting lane

### Wingers

* Move inside box edges.

---

## Step 4: Layoff Execution

```text id="j6v3qp"
ST → LCM or RCM
```

---

## Step 5: Shot Phase

* CM shoots first time OR
* Winger cuts inside OR
* Fullback overlaps for cross

---

# Pattern 5: Long Diagonal Direct Attack

---

## Step 1: Switch Trigger

CB identifies weak-side space.

---

## Step 2: Execution

```text id="x8t2lm"
LCB → RW
```

or

```text id="r3p6vq"
RCB → LW
```

---

## Step 3: Winger Reaction

### RW / LW

* Takes first touch forward.
* Attacks fullback immediately.

---

## Step 4: Opposite Side Movement

### Far winger

* Sprints far post.

### Striker

* Near-post attack.

---

### Midfielders

* Crash box centrally.

---

# Pattern 6: Route-One Direct Attack

Extreme version of direct play.

---

## Step 1: GK Launch

```text id="b7k9mz"
GK → ST
```

---

## Step 2: Striker Duel

* Aerial contest.
* Flick or hold.

---

## Step 3: Chaos Phase

* Ball drops unpredictably.
* Midfielders sprint forward.

---

## Step 4: Finishing Movement

* First attacker to ball shoots.
* Others support rebounds.

---

# Player Role Summary (Direct Attack Form 2)

## Goalkeeper

* Launches long passes.
* Starts direct attacks instantly.

## Center-Backs

* Skip buildup.
* Play vertical or diagonal passes.

## Fullbacks

* Provide width for second phase.
* Attack space after long ball.

## Defensive Midfielder

* Wins second balls.
* Recycles possession.

## Central Midfielders

* Arrive late into box.
* Attack rebounds and layoffs.

## Wingers

* Sprint into channels.
* Attack loose space immediately.

## Striker

* Key focal point:

  * Hold ball
  * Win aerial duels
  * Lay off
  * Attack space behind

---

# Core Principle of Direct Attacks

Instead of building through structure, the team creates:

```text id="g9v3xk"
LONG BALL → DUEL → SECOND BALL → SHOT
```

Everything is based on:

* Winning physical battles
* Winning second phases
* Arriving faster than the opponent
