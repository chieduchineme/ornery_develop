# Attack Form 3: Possession-Based Attack (4-3-3)

Possession-based attacking is not about speed. It's about **controlling space, manipulating opponents, creating superiorities, and waiting for the right moment to penetrate**.

A single possession can last 20–90 seconds and involve every player touching the ball.

---

# Pattern 1: U-Shape Circulation

## Objective

Move the opponent from side to side until a gap appears.

### Initial Shape

```text
      ST
LW          RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
       GK
```

---

## Goalkeeper (GK)

### Actions

* Acts as an extra outfield player.
* Receives back-passes.
* Switches play.

### Movement

* Constantly adjusts position behind ball.
* Creates passing angles.

### Event Trigger

When one side becomes blocked:

```text
LCB → GK → RCB
```

Restart attack.

---

## Left Center-Back (LCB)

### Actions

* Carries ball forward.
* Draws first presser.

### Movement

* Steps into midfield if unmarked.
* Moves wider when needed.

### Objective

Force opponent forward.

---

## Right Center-Back (RCB)

Mirror role.

Maintains width.

Receives switches.

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

### Most Important Player

### Movement

Constantly:

```text
↓
← DM →
↑
```

Creates passing angles.

Always tries to be:

* Behind pressure
* Between opponent midfielders

### Objective

Become the free player.

---

## Left Back (LB)

### Actions

Provides width.

### Movement

```text
Touchline
|
LB →
```

Pushes high.

Keeps opponent winger occupied.

---

## Right Back (RB)

Same role opposite side.

---

## Left Central Midfielder (LCM)

### Movement

Drops.

Rotates.

Advances.

Checks into pockets.

### Objective

Create triangles:

```text
LB
 \
  LCM
 /
DM
```

---

## Right Central Midfielder (RCM)

Same function opposite side.

---

## Left Winger (LW)

### Position

Stays wide.

### Objective

Stretch defense horizontally.

### Movement

```text
Touchline
|
LW
```

Keeps fullback isolated.

---

## Right Winger (RW)

Same.

---

## Striker (ST)

### Actions

Occupies center-backs.

### Movement

Checks:

```text
ST ↓
```

Then:

```text
ST ↑
```

Drops and spins.

Creates uncertainty.

---

## Full Sequence

### Seconds 1-10

LCB → DM → LB

Opponent shifts left.

### Seconds 10-20

LB → DM → RCB

Opponent shifts right.

### Seconds 20-25

Gap appears.

Pass through line.

Attack progresses.

# Pattern 2: Third-Man Combination

## Objective

Beat pressure without dribbling.

---

## Example

### Player A

DM

### Player B

RCM

### Player C

RW

---

### Movement

```text
DM → RCM

RW running
```

RCM receives.

One touch:

```text
RCM → RW
```

Opponent presses RCM.

RW becomes free.

---

## Detailed Roles

### DM

Draws pressure.

Passes.

Moves behind play.

---

### RCM

Acts as wall.

Rarely turns.

One-touch pass.

---

### RW

Times run perfectly.

Receives facing goal.

---

### Striker

Pins nearest center-back.

Creates corridor.

---

### LW

Maintains width.

Prevents defensive collapse.

---

# Pattern 3: Positional Rotation

## Objective

Confuse marking responsibilities.

---

## Left Side Rotation

### Initial

```text
LW

LCM

LB
```

---

### Rotation

LW moves inside.

LCM moves wide.

LB overlaps.

---

### New Shape

```text
LB

LW

LCM
```

Defender must decide:

* Follow?
* Pass on?
* Hold position?

Confusion creates space.

---

## LW Actions

### Before

Wide.

### During

Moves into half-space.

### After

Receives between lines.

---

## LCM Actions

Moves toward wing.

Receives.

Maintains possession.

---

## LB Actions

Explosive overlap.

Provides depth.

---

## Opposite Side

RB, RW, RCM mirror movement.

# Pattern 4: Numerical Overload

## Objective

Create more attackers than defenders.

Example:

```text
4 attackers vs 3 defenders
```

---

## Left-Side Overload

### Players Involved

LW

LCM

DM

---

### Shape

```text
LW

LCM

DM

LB
```

Four players in same zone.

---

## Defensive Reaction

* Fullback
* Winger
* Midfielder

---

## Result

Space opens elsewhere.

---

## Key Movements

Receives under pressure.

---

### LCM

Support angle.

---

### DM

Safety valve.

---

### LB

Overlap.

---

### ST

Occupies center-backs.

---

### RW

# Pattern 5: Switch-and-Attack

## Objective

Use overload to free opposite side.

---

## Phase 1

Overload left.

```text
LW
LCM
LB
DM
```

Defense shifts.

---

## Phase 2

Switch.

```text
DM → RCB → RB
```

or

```text
DM → RW
```

---

## RW Actions

Receives with space.

Attacks 1v1.

---

## RB Actions

Overlap.

---

## ST Actions

Near-post run.

---

## LW Actions

Far-post run.

---

## Midfielders

Attack edge of box.

# Pattern 6: Positional Play (Elite Version)

Associated with Pep Guardiola.

---

## Structure

Five lanes:

```text
LW  LHS  ST  RHS  RW
```

(Half-space lanes between center and wing)

---

## Player Positions

### LW

Far left lane.

### RW

Far right lane.

### ST

Central lane.

### LCM

Left half-space.

### RCM

Right half-space.

---

## Objective

Occupy every lane.

Stretch defense.

Create free man.

---

## Sequence

### Step 1

Back line circulates.

### Step 2

DM attracts pressure.

### Step 3

LCM becomes free.

### Step 4

LCM receives.

### Step 5

ST pins CB.

### Step 6

LW attacks behind.

### Step 7

Through ball.

### Step 8

Cutback or shot.

---

# What Every Player Is Constantly Doing in Possession Play

### Goalkeeper

* Creating numerical superiority.
* Recycling possession.

### Center-Backs

* Progressing ball.
* Drawing press.

### Defensive Midfielder

* Connecting all phases.
* Finding free players.

### Fullbacks

* Providing width or underlaps.

### Central Midfielders

* Creating triangles.
* Finding pockets.

### Wingers

* Stretching defense.
* Isolating defenders.

### Striker

* Pinning center-backs.
* Creating depth.
* Opening spaces for teammates.

The key principle is that possession attacks are less about individual actions and more about **creating and exploiting space through coordinated movement**. Every player's position affects every other player's options, so even a player who never touches the ball may be performing a crucial role by dragging a defender, occupying a lane, or maintaining team structure.
