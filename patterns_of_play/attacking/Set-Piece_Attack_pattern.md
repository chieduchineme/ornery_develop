# Attack Form: Set-Piece Attack (Corners, Free Kicks, Throw-ins, Penalties)

Set-pieces are the most **pre-planned, choreographed attacking phase** in football. Unlike open play, every player has a **scripted movement pattern triggered by the delivery**.

Set-pieces are built on:

* blocking and screening
* timed runs (not reactive runs)
* zone targeting (near post, far post, penalty spot)
* second-ball structures
* rebound positioning

I’ll break this into major set-piece types and show **exact movement of all 11 players step-by-step**.

---

# 1) CORNER KICK ATTACK

## Base Structure (Typical 4-3-3 attacking corner)

```text id="sp1"
        ST
LW               RW

   LCM   RCM

        DM

LB  LCB  RCB  RB
```

* 1 taker (usually RW or LCM)

---

# Pattern 1: Near-Post Flick Corner

## Step 1: Setup Phase

### Key attackers

* ST → near-post zone
* LCM → edge of 6-yard box
* RCM → penalty spot
* LW/RW → far-post run

---

## Step 2: Movement Trigger (Corner taken)

### ST

* Sprints toward near post
* Arrives slightly ahead of defender

```text id="sp2"
corner → ST ↗ near post
```

---

### LCM

* Starts behind ST
* Curved run to block keeper’s path

---

### DM

* Stays top of box (second ball zone)

---

### Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* One stays back (rest defense)
* One attacks edge of box

---

## Step 3: Delivery Action

Ball is aimed:

* near-post head height

---

## Step 4: Contact Phase

### ST

* Flicks ball across goal

### RCM

* Attacks penalty spot for rebound

### LW/RW

* Far-post tap-in run

---

## Step 5: Outcome Structure

```text id="sp3"
Near post → flick → far post finish
```

---

# Pattern 2: Far-Post Overload Corner

---

## Step 1: Setup

Heavy load far post:

* LW + LCM + ST cluster

---

## Step 2: Movement

### LW

* Curved run behind far defender

### ST

* Blocks goalkeeper sight line

### LCM

* Arrives late at back post

---

## Step 3: Delivery

Ball aimed:

* high, deep far post

---

## Step 4: Execution

* Header back across goal OR
* Direct far-post header

---

## Step 5: Rebound Structure

* DM + RB stay outside box for second ball

---

# Pattern 3: Edge-of-Box Shot Corner

---

## Step 1: Box Chaos Setup

Most players inside box except DM + RCM

---

## Step 2: Movement

* ST + CBs attack aerial zone
* LW/RW make decoy runs toward near post

---

## Step 3: Set Play Trigger

Ball is **delayed or short corner**

```text id="sp4"
corner → short pass → DM/RCM
```

---

## Step 4: Strike Phase

* RCM receives outside box
* One-touch or controlled shot

---

## Step 5: Box Positioning

* ST screens keeper
* LW/RW block defenders

---

# Pattern 4: Short Corner Combination

---

## Step 1: Setup

```text id="sp5"
Corner taker + RW + RB triangle
```

---

## Step 2: Movement

### RW

* Drops to receive short pass

### RB

* Overlaps instantly

### ST

* Moves toward near post

---

## Step 3: Combination Flow

```text id="sp6"
Corner → RW → RB → cross
```

---

## Step 4: Box Attack

* ST near post
* LW far post
* CM penalty spot

---

# 2) FREE KICK ATTACK (Wide + Central)

---

# Pattern 1: Direct Shot Free Kick

---

## Step 1: Setup

* 1 shooter (RCM or LCM)
* 3 blockers (ST + LW + RW)

---

## Step 2: Movement

### ST

* Blocks wall sight line

### Wingers

* Run off wall to distract

---

## Step 3: Execution

* Direct strike on goal

---

# Pattern 2: Free Kick Cross Attack

---

## Step 1: Setup

```text id="sp7"
ST central
LCM/RWM runners
```

---

## Step 2: Movement

* ST attacks near post
* LW/RW attack far post
* CBs join box

---

## Step 3: Delivery

Ball aimed:

* between penalty spot and 6-yard box

---

## Step 4: Outcome

* header
* volley
* rebound finish

---

# Pattern 3: Free Kick Layoff Shot

---

## Step 1: Setup

* 1 player stands over ball
* 1 player nearby (layoff option)

---

## Step 2: Movement

```text id="sp8"
pass → layoff → shot
```

---

## Step 3: Attackers

* ST blocks wall
* RW/LCM takes shot

---

# 3) THROW-IN ATTACK

---

# Pattern 1: Quick Throw + Overload

---

## Step 1: Setup

```text id="sp9"
LW + LB + LCM triangle
```

---

## Step 2: Movement

* LB shows short option
* LCM drops into pocket
* LW stays high

---

## Step 3: Execution

```text id="sp10"
Throw → LB → LCM → LW
```

---

## Step 4: Attack Phase

* LW drives forward
* ST attacks box

---

# Pattern 2: Long Throw Attack

---

## Step 1: Setup

* LB or CB takes long throw
* Everyone enters box

---

## Step 2: Movement

* ST attacks near post
* CBs attack central zone
* CM arrives late

---

## Step 3: Delivery

Ball launched into penalty area like a corner

---

## Step 4: Chaos Phase

* Flick-ons
* Second balls
* Scramble finishing

---

# 4) PENALTY ATTACK STRUCTURE (Rebound System)

---

## Step 1: Penalty Setup

* ST ready near rebound zone
* RW/LW positioned at edge of box

---

## Step 2: Movement Rules

### Before kick

* All attackers stay outside box

---

## Step 3: If rebound occurs

```text id="sp11"
ball rebound → fastest attacker reacts
```

---

## Step 4: Attackers

* ST = central rebound
* Wingers = side rebounds
* CM = top of box shot

---

# PLAYER ROLE SUMMARY (ALL SET-PIECES)

---

## Striker (ST)

* Primary aerial target
* Near-post runner
* Blocker of goalkeeper sight
* Rebound attacker

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* Far-post runners
* Short-corner participants
* Rebound attackers
* Decoy runners

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Edge-of-box shooters
* Late runners into box
* Second-ball collectors

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Stays outside box
* Prevents counterattacks
* Controls second phase

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* One stays back (rest defense)
* One attacks edge of box or overlaps short corner

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Main aerial attackers
* Screen goalkeeper
* Attack central zones

---

# CORE PRINCIPLE OF SET-PIECE ATTACK

Set-pieces are not random—they are:

```text id="sp12"
TIMED RUNS + FIXED ZONES + SCREENING + SECOND BALL STRUCTURE
```
