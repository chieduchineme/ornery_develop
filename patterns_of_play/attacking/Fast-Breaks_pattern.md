# Attack Form 2: Fast Breaks (4-3-3)

A **fast break** is different from a counter-attack.

* **Counter-attack:** exploits an opponent caught out after losing possession.
* **Fast break:** any rapid attack where the team advances the ball at maximum speed before the defense can organize, even from a goal kick, save, rebound, interception, or restart.

The goal is usually to reach a shooting opportunity within **5–12 seconds**.

I'll use a standard 4-3-3 and cover the main fast-break patterns.

---

# Pattern 1: Central Fast Break

## Situation

The ball is recovered in midfield.

```text
      ST
LW         RW

   LCM  RCM
      DM

LB CB CB RB
```

The opponent's midfield is temporarily disorganized.

---

# Second 0–1: Ball Recovery

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

Wins the ball.

### Immediate actions

1. Secure first touch.
2. Lift head.
3. Scan runners.

### Decision

Looks for:

* Striker run
* Midfielder run
* Winger run

### Movement

Takes first touch forward.

Never sideways if space exists.

---

## Center-Backs

### Ball-side CB

Pushes forward 5–10 meters.

Provides support.

### Far-side CB

Slides centrally.

Prepares for loss of possession.

---

## Goalkeeper

Moves higher.

Acts as sweeper.

Prepares for long clearance if attack fails.

---

# Second 1–3: Expansion Phase

## Striker (ST)

### Initial movement

Checks defensive line.

Looks for:

```text
CB      CB
   ST
```

Gap between center-backs.

### Run options

#### Straight penetration

```text
ST ↑
```

#### Curved run

```text
ST ↗
```

Avoids offside.

#### Decoy run

Pulls defenders away.

---

## Left Winger (LW)

### Immediate reaction

Full sprint.

Attacks channel.

```text
LW ↗
```

### Objective

Stretch back line.

Force fullback to retreat.

---

## Right Winger (RW)

Mirror movement.

```text
RW ↖
```

Creates maximum width.

---

# Second 3–5: Midfield Support

## Left Central Midfielder (LCM)

### First movement

Forward sprint.

Targets:

```text
Penalty Arc
```

### Purpose

Become:

* Third-man option
* Rebound collector
* Late scorer

---

## Right Central Midfielder (RCM)

Same role.

Usually attacks opposite half-space.

---

# Penetration Event

DM finds ST.

```text
DM → ST
```

---

## ST Receives

### Option A

Turn and shoot.

### Option B

Lay off.

### Option C

Slip through ball.

---

# Team Movements

### LW

Attacks far-post lane.

### RW

Attacks opposite lane.

### LCM

Arrives top of box.

### RCM

Supports behind.

---

# Pattern 2: Wide Fast Break

Most common in modern football.

---

## Ball Recovery

Often by:

* Fullback
* Winger
* Midfielder

Near touchline.

---

## Ball-Side Fullback

Wins ball.

### Immediate action

Forward pass.

Not dribbling.

Not recycling.

Forward immediately.

---

## Ball-Side Winger

Receives.

### First touch

Forward.

### Objective

Attack retreating defense.

---

# Supporting Movements

## Striker

Runs near-post corridor.

```text
ST →
```

Occupies center-backs.

---

## Opposite Winger

Attacks far post.

```text
RW →
```

Critical scoring run.

---

## Near-Side Midfielder

Supports underneath.

```text
LCM →
```

Creates passing triangle.

---

## Far-Side Midfielder

Late run.

Targets penalty spot.

---

## Opposite Fullback

Moves cautiously.

Provides balance.

---

# Finishing Structure

```text
Far Post   RW

   ST

Penalty Spot LCM
```

Three separate targets.

---

# Pattern 3: Three-Lane Fast Break

Elite teams use this constantly.

---

# Structure

```text
LW     ST     RW
```

Three vertical lanes.

---

## Ball Carrier

Usually DM or CM.

### Objective

Keep defenders uncertain.

Carries ball centrally.

---

## LW

Runs left channel.

---

## ST

Runs central channel.

---

## RW

Runs right channel.

---

# Defender Problem

Three attackers.

Two center-backs.

One recovering midfielder.

Who marks whom?

---

# Passing Triggers

### If CB steps to ST

Pass wide.

### If Fullback narrows

Pass opposite side.

### If defense stays deep

Ball carrier shoots.

---

# Pattern 4: Goalkeeper-Led Fast Break

Begins after save.

---

## Goalkeeper

Catches shot.

### Immediately scans

Finds:

* Winger
* Striker
* Fullback

### Distribution

#### Throw

```text
GK → LW
```

#### Long kick

```text
GK → ST
```

#### Side volley

Into space.

---

## LW

Receives running.

Not stationary.

Critical principle:

Receive while moving.

---

## ST

Starts run before ball released.

---

## RW

Explodes forward simultaneously.

---

## Midfielders

Sprint through center.

Provide second wave.

---

# Pattern 5: Rebound Fast Break

Very common after defending a set piece.

---

## First Ball

Defender clears.

---

## Striker

Moves toward loose ball.

---

## Wingers

Already sprinting outward.

```text
LW ← clearance → RW
```

Creating width.

---

## DM

Recovers second ball.

Immediately plays forward.

---

## Entire Team

Pushes 20–30 meters up field together.

---

# Pattern 6: Overload-to-Break

A modern positional-play fast break.

---

## Recovery

Ball won in crowded area.

Example:

```text
LB
LW
LCM
DM
```

Four players close together.

---

## Immediate Combination

```text
DM → LCM

LCM → LW
```

One-touch passing.

---

## LW Escapes Pressure

Now attacking open field.

---

## Supporting Runs

### ST

Central run.

### RW

Far-side sprint.

### LCM

Underlap.

### LB

Overlap.

---

# Pattern 7: Direct Vertical Fast Break

The most aggressive version.

---

## Recovery

CB intercepts.

---

## CB

Immediately:

```text
CB → ST
```

No midfield involvement.

---

## ST

One-touch layoff.

```text
ST → LCM
```

---

## LCM

Through ball.

```text
LCM → LW
```

---

## LW

Enters box.

Shoots or cuts back.

---

# Movement Summary by Position

## Goalkeeper

* Quick distribution.
* Higher supporting position.

## Center-Backs

* First pass forward.
* Rest-defense coverage.

## Fullbacks

* Sprint support.
* Provide width or overlap.

## Defensive Midfielder

* Launch attacks.
* Protect against turnovers.

## Central Midfielders

* Arrive late.
* Support combinations.
* Attack rebounds.

## Wingers

* Maximum sprint width.
* Attack channels.
* Far-post runs.

## Striker

* Stretch defense.
* Pin center-backs.
* Create passing options.
