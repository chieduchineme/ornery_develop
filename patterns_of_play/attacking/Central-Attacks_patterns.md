# Attack Form: Central Attack (4-3-3)

Central attacking is about **penetrating through the middle third of the pitch**, especially between and behind the opponent’s midfield and defensive lines. It relies on **tight combinations, vertical passes, third-man runs, and half-space occupation** rather than width.

I'll break it into key central attack patterns and show the **movement of all 11 players step-by-step**.

---

# Pattern 1: Vertical Line-Break Through the Middle

## Situation

Ball is in midfield and opponent is in a compact shape.

```text id="c1"
      ST
LW           RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
```

---

## Step 1: Build Phase

### Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Drops between CBs or just ahead of them.
* Receives first pass.

```text id="c2"
LCB → DM ← RCB
```

### Movement

* Constant scanning.
* Turns forward if space exists.

---

## Step 2: Midfield Positioning

### LCM + RCM

* Split into half-spaces.

```text id="c3"
LCM   ↑   RCM
   (between lines)
```

### Movement

* One checks short.
* One stays high.

Creates vertical passing lanes.

---

## Step 3: Striker Movement

### ST

Two options:

#### Option A: Drop

```text id="c4"
ST ↓
```

* Pulls CB out of line.

#### Option B: Pin

* Stays high to stretch defense vertically.

---

## Step 4: Penetration Pass

### DM or CB

```text id="c5"
DM → LCM or ST
```

* Direct vertical pass into midfield gap.

---

## Step 5: Final Third Entry

Once ball enters central zone:

### LCM / RCM actions

* Turn quickly
* Play through ball or carry forward

### ST actions

* Spins behind defense

### Wingers

* Move inward (not wide)
* Attack half-spaces

---

## End Structure

```text id="c6"
     LW ST RW
       LCM RCM
          DM
```

All players converge centrally.

---

# Pattern 2: Through-Ball Penetration Attack

---

## Step 1: Midfield Control

Ball with CM or DM.

---

## Step 2: Striker Movement

### ST

* Starts on defensive line.
* Then makes timed run.

```text id="c7"
CB ---- CB
      ST → (run)
```

---

## Step 3: Midfield Trigger

### LCM / RCM

* Look for split pass.

```text id="c8"
CM → ST
```

or

```text id="c9"
CM → winger inside run
```

---

## Step 4: Supporting Runs

### Wingers

* Cut inside immediately
* Attack channels behind fullbacks

### Opposite winger

* Far-post diagonal run

---

## Step 5: Final Action

* Through ball between CBs
* Or diagonal pass into channel

---

# Pattern 3: False 9 Central Disruption

---

## Step 1: Striker Drops Deep

```text id="c10"
ST ↓
```

Pulls one or both CBs forward.

---

## Step 2: Midfield Exploitation

### LCM / RCM

* Attack space behind CB line.

```text id="c11"
LCM ↑
RCM ↑
```

---

## Step 3: Winger Inversion

### LW / RW

* Move inside toward goal.

```text id="c12"
LW → central
RW → central
```

---

## Step 4: Central Overload

Now midfield + attack form overload:

```text id="c13"
   LW  LCM  ST  RCM  RW
```

---

## Step 5: Penetration

Options:

* ST lays off → CM shoots
* CM runs through → shot
* Winger receives through ball

---

# Pattern 4: Third-Man Central Combination

---

## Step 1: Build Triangle

Example:

```text id="c14"
DM → LCM → ST
```

---

## Step 2: Movement

### DM

* Passes and stays behind

### LCM

* One-touch layoff

### ST

* Runs beyond defensive line

---

## Step 3: Key Idea

Ball never goes directly to final attacker.

Instead:

```text id="c15"
A → B → C (free run)
```

---

## Step 4: Result

* ST receives behind defense
* Or CM arrives into shooting zone

---

# Pattern 5: Half-Space Central Attack

---

## Step 1: Positioning

### LCM and RCM

Move into:

```text id="c16"
| LB | LHS | ST | RHS | RB |
```

(Half-space = between wing and center)

---

## Step 2: Ball Entry

```text id="c17"
DM → LCM (half-space)
```

---

## Step 3: Movement Synchronization

### ST

* Pins CBs

### LW/RW

* Stay wide or make diagonal runs

### CM (opposite side)

* Arrives at edge of box

---

## Step 4: Penetration Options

* Through ball to ST
* Cutback to CM
* Switch to winger

---

# Pattern 6: Box Midfield Central Attack

---

## Structure

```text id="c18"
   LCM   RCM
      DM
   ST
```

---

## Step 1: Build

CBs pass into DM.

---

## Step 2: Central Triangle Formation

LCM and RCM move close to DM.

Creates tight triangle for quick combinations.

---

## Step 3: Movement Flow

* Constant 1–2 touch passing
* Rotations between CM and DM

---

## Step 4: Penetration Trigger

One midfielder breaks line:

```text id="c19"
LCM → forward run
```

---

## Step 5: Final Phase

* ST receives or lays off
* CM arrives for shot
* Wingers attack inside lanes

---

# Pattern 7: Direct Central Smash Attack

---

## Step 1: Vertical Pass

```text id="c20"
CB → ST
```

---

## Step 2: Striker Action

* Holds ball
* Shields under pressure

---

## Step 3: Midfield Crash

```text id="c21"
LCM + RCM → box
```

---

## Step 4: Second Phase

* Layoff → shot
* Flick-on → runner
* Loose ball → scramble

---

# Player Role Summary (Central Attack Form)

## Striker (ST)

* Pins center-backs
* Drops into midfield
* Runs behind defense
* Plays wall passes

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Break defensive lines
* Make late runs into box
* Create through balls
* Combine in tight spaces

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Controls tempo
* Vertical passing hub
* Protects against loss of possession

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* In central attacks, they:

  * Invert into half-spaces
  * Become second strikers
  * Provide diagonal runs

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* Usually stay deeper
* Or tuck inside to support midfield

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Initiate vertical passes
* Step into midfield when space opens

---

# Core Principle of Central Attack

Central attack is about breaking compact defenses through:

```text id="c22"
VERTICAL PASS → MOVEMENT BEHIND LINES → QUICK FINISH
```

It relies on:

* Tight spacing
* Timing runs
* Third-man combinations
* Exploiting half-space gaps
