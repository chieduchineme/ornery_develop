# Attack Form: Underlapping Attacks (4-3-3)

Underlapping attacks are the **inside-channel version of overlaps**. Instead of running around the winger on the outside, the runner attacks **inside the winger, into the half-space or central channel behind the defensive line**.

They are especially powerful because:

* they break compact blocks from the inside (harder to defend than wide)
* they exploit blind-side movement (defenders see the ball, not the runner)
* they create cutback and central shooting lanes instead of crosses

I’ll break it into major patterns and show **full 11-player movement step-by-step**.

---

# Pattern 1: Classic Underlap (Fullback Inside Run)

## Situation

Ball is on wing in structured attack.

```text id="u1"
      ST
LW           RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
```

Assume left side attack.

---

## Step 1: Winger Fixes Wide Defender

### LW actions

* Holds width OR dribbles slightly outward
* Forces fullback to commit wide

```text id="u2"
LW ↔ LB opponent stretched wide
```

Key idea:

> Defender is pulled OUTSIDE → space opens INSIDE

---

## Step 2: Underlap Trigger

### LB movement (key action)

* Starts from deep position
* Runs INSIDE the winger, not outside
* Targets half-space channel behind defense

```text id="u3"
LB → inside LW → half-space run
```

This is the underlap corridor:

* between CB and fullback
* or inside fullback channel

---

## Step 3: Midfield Support Structure

### LCM

* Moves into top-of-box pocket
* Becomes rebound/shooting option

### DM

* Holds central balance
* Covers counterattack zone

### RCM

* Shifts across for far-side occupation

---

## Step 4: Striker Movement

### ST

* Curves run between CBs
* Or pins both CBs centrally

Purpose:

* prevent CBs from stepping into underlap lane

---

## Step 5: Winger Decision Phase

### LW now has options:

* slip pass into underlapping LB
* cut inside and shoot
* recycle to LCM

---

## Step 6: Final Action

### Most common outcomes:

* LB receives in half-space → low cutback
* LW → LB → quick 1-2 into box
* LB → shot from inside channel

---

## Final Shape

```text id="u4"
     ST
LB  LCM   RW
```

# Pattern 2: Inverted Winger + Underlap System (Modern Build)

---

## Step 1: Winger Moves Inside

### LW movement

* shifts into half-space
* acts like playmaker or striker

```text id="u5"
LW → central channel
```

---

## Step 2: Fullback Provides Inside Depth

### LB movement

* pushes into central corridor behind winger

```text id="u6"
LB → inside run beyond LW
```

---

## Step 3: Midfield Rotation

### LCM

* moves slightly wider or stays central depending on space

### DM

* holds defensive balance

---

## Step 4: Striker Behavior

### ST

* pins CBs
* prevents them from stepping into LB’s run lane

---

## Step 5: Execution

* LW attracts defenders centrally
* LB arrives late into inside channel
* pass splits CB and fullback

---

# Pattern 3: Third-Man Underlap (Most Elite Pattern)

---

## Core Idea

The runner does NOT receive the ball directly.

Instead:

> Player A → Player B → Underlapping Player C

---

## Step 1: Triangle Formation

```text id="u7"
LW → LCM → LB
```

---

## Step 2: Movement Roles

### LW

* holds defender wide

### LCM

* plays one-touch pass inside

### LB

* starts run AFTER pass is released

---

## Step 3: Timing Principle

LB arrives:

* after defender shifts attention away
* into blind-side channel

---

## Step 4: Final Action

* LB receives inside box channel
* low cross or shot

---

# Pattern 4: Double Underlap (Midfield + Fullback Collapse)

---

## Step 1: Wing Fixation

```text id="u8"
LW isolated wide
```

---

## Step 2: Dual Inside Runs

### LB

* underlaps into half-space

### LCM

* also underlaps centrally

```text id="u9"
LB → inside lane
LCM → central lane
```

---

## Step 3: Winger Decision Pressure

LW has:

* LB inside option
* LCM central option
* dribble option

---

## Step 4: Defensive Collapse

Opponent cannot track both inside runners.

---

## Step 5: Outcome

* LB receives for cutback
* or LCM shoots from box edge

---

# Pattern 5: Underlap to Cutback Zone Attack

LB → inside run behind defense

# Player Role Summary (Underlapping Attacks)

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* Primary underlapping runners
* Attack inside channels behind winger
* Time runs after winger fixes defender
* Provide surprise central penetration

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* Stretch width OR drift inside to create confusion
* Fix defenders wide
* Trigger underlap timing
* Decide final pass or shot

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Provide inside support lanes
* Act as third-man passers
* Arrive late into box for shots

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Maintains structure during inside runs
* Prevents counterattacks
* Switches play if underlap is blocked

---

## Striker (ST)

* Pins center-backs
* Creates central gravity
* Opens inside lanes for underlapping runs

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Initiate buildup
* Provide diagonal switches
* Step into midfield if space opens

---

# Core Principle of Underlapping Attacks

Underlapping is about:

```text id="u15"
WIDE FIXATION → INSIDE RUN → BLIND-SIDE EXPLOITATION → CUTBACK OR CENTRAL FINISH
```

Key advantages:

* harder to track than overlaps
* breaks compact defenses from inside
* creates higher xG chances (cutbacks, central shots)
