# Attack Form: Switch of Play (4-3-3)

Switch of play is about **intentionally shifting the ball from one congested area to a free zone on the opposite side**, using **width, timing, scanning, and coordinated off-ball movement**.

Unlike overloads (which *create pressure on purpose*), switches are about:

* **recognizing overload pressure**
* **drawing defenders to one side**
* **escaping instantly to the weak side**

A switch is never just a pass—it is a **team-wide repositioning event**.

---

# Pattern 1: Build-Up-to-Switch (Controlled Circulation Switch)

## Situation

Team is building on the left side.

```text id="s1"
      ST
LW           RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
```

Ball is on the left.

---

## Step 1: Left-Side Overload Forms (Intentional)

### Involved players:

* LW
* LB
* LCM
* DM

They combine to attract pressure.

```text id="s2"
LW + LB + LCM + DM
```

---

## Step 2: Opponent Compressed Reaction

Defensive shift:

* Right winger tucks in
* Midfield slides left
* Fullback steps up

Result: **left side becomes crowded**

---

## Step 3: DM Becomes Switch Hub

### DM movement

* Drops slightly deeper
* Opens body shape
* Scans opposite wing constantly

---

## Step 4: Switch Trigger

```text id="s3"
DM → RB or RW
```

* Long diagonal pass
* Or fast driven ground switch

---

## Step 5: Opposite Side Explosion

### RW movement

* Stays wide and high
* Receives in isolation

```text id="s4"
RW 1v1 vs LB
```

---

### RB movement

* Overlaps immediately
* Creates 2v1 overload

---

### ST movement

* Attacks central lane (near post)

---

### LCM movement

* Late arrival into box (far post zone)

---

## End Structure

```text id="s5"
LW   ST   RW
     LCM
         RB
```

Weak side fully exposed.

---

# Pattern 2: Back-Line Switch (CB-to-CB or CB-to-Far-Wing)

---

## Situation

Ball circulated between defenders under light pressure.

---

## Step 1: Defensive Shape

Opponent presses one side.

```text id="s6"
Pressing LW side
```

---

## Step 2: CB Actions

### Ball-side CB

* Carries forward slightly
* Attracts press

### Far CB

* Moves wide and deep to open angle

---

## Step 3: Switch Execution

```text id="s7"
LCB → RCB → RB or RW
```

or direct:

```text id="s8"
LCB → RW (diagonal)
```

---

## Step 4: Movement Synchronization

### RW

* Starts wide touchline position
* Ready for 1v1

### RB

* High overlap run
* Arrives after switch is already in progress

---

### Midfielders

* Shift across pitch instantly
* LCM becomes support on weak side
* RCM attacks half-space

---

## Result

Opponent is still shifting → RW receives unpressured.

---

# Pattern 3: Midfield Pivot Switch (DM-Controlled Switch)

---

## Objective

Use DM as central “switch engine”.

---

## Step 1: Central Compression

Opponent presses midfield heavily.

---

## Step 2: DM Receives Under Pressure

```text id="s9"
CB → DM
```

---

## Step 3: DM Rotation Movement

* Opens body toward weak side
* Takes 1–2 touches max
* Scans opposite winger

---

## Step 4: Switch Pass

```text id="s10"
DM → LW or RW
```

Often diagonal aerial pass.

---

## Step 5: Team Movements

### Weak side winger

* Already positioned high and wide
* First touch forward

### Opposite winger

* Becomes far-post runner

### Striker

* Central run into box

### Fullback

* Late overlap support

---

# Pattern 4: Rapid Counter-Switch (Transition Switch)

---

## Situation

Ball recovered on one side during transition.

---

## Step 1: Recovery Zone

Example:

```text id="s11"
LB wins ball
```

---

## Step 2: Immediate Decision

Instead of advancing same side:

```text id="s12"
LB → RW (instant switch)
```

---

## Step 3: Movement Triggers

### RW

* Already sprinting forward
* Receives in open space

### LW

* Becomes far-post attacker

### ST

* Runs central lane

### Midfielders

* Sprint into box late

---

## Key Idea

No buildup phase—just:

> WIN → LOOK → SWITCH → EXPLODE

---

# Pattern 5: Switch-to-Isolation Attack

---

## Objective

Create 1v1 on weak side intentionally.

---

## Step 1: Overload Side A

```text id="s13"
LW + LB + LCM
```

---

## Step 2: Opponent Collapse

Defense shifts heavily.

---

## Step 3: Switch Execution

```text id="s14"
LCM → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Isolation Moment

### RW receives:

```text id="s15"
RW vs LB (isolated 1v1)
```

No immediate support nearby.

---

## Step 5: Supporting Runs

* ST attacks near post
* LCM arrives top of box
* LB (opposite side) stays deeper

---

# Pattern 6: Switch During Final Third Entry

---

## Situation

Team already near penalty area.

---

## Step 1: Wing Attack Collapses

```text id="s16"
LW blocked by 2 defenders
```

---

## Step 2: Cutback or Recycle

```text id="s17"
LW → DM or LCM
```

---

## Step 3: Immediate Switch

```text id="s18"
LCM → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Box Movement

### ST

* Near-post run

### RW

* Cross or cut inside

### LW

* Far-post recovery run

---

# Player Role Summary (Switch of Play Form)

---

## Goalkeeper (GK)

* Acts as emergency switch outlet
* Initiates diagonal switches when pressed
* Maintains positional depth for recycling

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Primary switch initiators
* Draw press before releasing ball
* Execute long diagonals across pitch

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Central switch hub
* Controls timing of switches
* Reads overload collapse
* Finds weak-side winger

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Provide intermediate passing lanes
* Support switches by repositioning quickly
* Arrive late on weak side for shots

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* Either create overload OR become weak-side attackers
* Provide width after switch
* Execute overlaps after switch completion

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* Key targets of switches
* Must stay wide and high
* Receive 1v1 situations
* Immediately attack space

---

## Striker (ST)

* Central anchor during switch
* Attacks box while ball is switching
* Times near-post and channel runs

---

# Core Principle of Switch of Play

Switching is based on a repeating cycle:

```text id="s19"
OVERLOAD → PRESSURE → SCANNING → SWITCH → EXPLOIT SPACE
```

Key ideas:

* One side is deliberately crowded
* Opponent shifts toward it
* Weak side becomes empty
* Ball is moved instantly into that space
* Attack is completed before defensive recovery
