# Attack Form: Wing Play (4-3-3)

Wing play is about **creating width, isolating wide defenders, and producing high-quality deliveries into the box**. Unlike direct play (Form 2), the buildup can be structured or semi-structured—but the final action is always **a wide-area penetration + box occupation**.

I’ll break it into the main wing-play patterns and show **every player’s movement step-by-step**.

---

# Pattern 1: Classic Overlap Wing Attack

## Situation

Ball goes to winger in wide area.

```text id="w1"
      ST
LW           RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
```

---

## Step 1: Ball to Winger

### LW receives near touchline

```text id="w2"
LW → receives wide
```

### LW actions

* First touch forward
* Faces fullback 1v1
* Draws defender toward him

---

## Step 2: Fullback Overlap

### LB movement

```text id="w3"
LB sprint → outside LW
```

### LB actions

* Explosive forward run
* Runs outside channel
* Creates 2v1 vs fullback

---

## Step 3: Midfield Support

### LCM

* Moves into half-space edge of box
* Ready for cutback

### DM

* Stays top of D (rebound zone)

### RCM

* Arrives opposite half-space for second ball

---

## Step 4: Striker Movement

### ST actions

* Near-post run
* Pins center-backs
* Prevents collapse to winger

---

## Step 5: Final Action Options

### Option A: Cross

```text id="w4"
LB/LW → cross into box
```

Targets:

* Near post (ST)
* Penalty spot (CM)
* Far post (RW)

---

### Option B: Cutback

```text id="w5"
LW → cutback → LCM/DM
```

High probability chance.

---

# Pattern 2: Underlap Wing Attack

---

## Step 1: Winger Holds Width

### LW

* Stays on touchline
* Freezes fullback

---

## Step 2: Underlapping Run

### LCM or LB

```text id="w6"
LCM → inside run behind winger
```

or

```text id="w7"
LB → inside channel run
```

---

## Step 3: Key Movement

### LW

* Delays pass
* Waits for runner

### Underlapper

* Runs into half-space behind defense

---

## Step 4: Striker + RW Movement

* ST attacks near post
* RW attacks far post

---

## Step 5: Final Action

* Slip pass into underlapper
* Low cutback or shot

---

# Pattern 3: Isolation Wing Attack (1v1 Focus)

---

## Step 1: Create Isolation

### Team structure shifts:

```text id="w8"
LW  (1v1 space created)
```

* LB drops deeper
* LCM moves inside
* ST central

---

## Step 2: Winger Receives

### LW actions

* Faces defender directly
* Tries dribble or feint

---

## Step 3: Support Movements

### LB

* Stays behind as safety pass

### LCM

* Arrives top of box

### DM

* Covers counterattack

---

## Step 4: Penetration

* Beat defender → cross or cut inside
* Or pass back → recycle → re-isolate

# Pattern 5: Early Cross Attack

---

## Step 1: Quick Wide Entry

```text id="w11"
CB → LW
```

or

```text id="w12"
DM → RW
```

---

## Step 2: Immediate Cross Trigger

### LW/RW actions

* No dribbling
* Cross within 1–2 touches

---

## Step 3: Box Movements

### ST

```text id="w13"
ST → near post run
```

### Opposite winger

```text id="w14"
RW → far post run
```

### Midfielders

* LCM + RCM → penalty spot crashes

---

## Step 4: Cross Delivery Zones

* Early cross = behind defense line
* ST attacks first touch zone
* CM attacks rebound zone

---

# Pattern 6: Byline Attack (Deep Wing Penetration)

---

## Step 1: Winger Beats Defender

```text id="w15"
LW → sprint to byline
```

---

## Step 2: Box Occupation

### ST

* Near post

### RW

* Far post

### CM

* Penalty spot

---

## Step 3: Decision Moment

LW has three options:

### 1. Cutback

```text id="w16"
LW → backward pass
```

### 2. Low cross

Across 6-yard box

### 3. Pull-back

To edge of box for shooter

# Pattern 8: Double-Wing Attack

---

## Structure

Both wings active:

```text id="w19"
LW           RW
LB           RB
```

---

## Step 1: Simultaneous Progression

* LW attacks left
* RW attacks right

---

## Step 2: Box Loading

# Player Role Summary (Wing Play Form)

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* Stretch pitch width
* 1v1 isolation
* Dribble or cross
* Cut inside for shot

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* Overlap runs
* Underlap surprise runs
* Provide crossing depth

---

## Striker (ST)

* Near-post runs
* Center-back pinning
* Flick-ons and rebounds

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Late box arrivals
* Edge-of-box shooting zone
* Combination support

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Rest defense
* Recycle possession
* Cover counterattacks

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Switch play
* High diagonal passes
* Maintain structure behind attack

---

# Core Principle of Wing Play

Wing play is built on 3 simultaneous forces:

```text id="w20"
WIDTH + OVERLOAD + BOX OCCUPATION
```

* Width: winger isolates defender
* Overload: fullback/CM joins
* Box occupation: 3–5 attackers attack cross zone
