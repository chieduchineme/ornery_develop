# Attack Form 9: High Press Attacking Patterns (Press → Steal → Immediate Scoring)

High-press attacking is the most **aggressive and shortest-path attacking form** in football.

It is not “build-up football.”
It is not “counter-attack after deep recovery.”

It is:

> **winning the ball inside or near the opponent’s defensive structure and attacking within 1–6 seconds**

The entire system is built on:

* coordinated pressing traps
* forcing predictable passes
* immediate vertical exploitation after recovery

I’ll break it into main patterns and show **exact movement of all 11 players during press → steal → finish**.

---

# Pattern 1: Touchline Press Trap (Wing Lock → Ball Win → Cross/Shot)

## Situation

Opponent tries to build from their right side.

```text id="p1"
        ST
LW             RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
```

---

## Step 1: Press Trigger

Ball goes to opponent fullback.

### LW movement

* Sprints to close passing lane inside
* Forces play outward

### ST movement

* Blocks pass back to CB
* Curves run to cut central option

---

## Step 2: Trap Formation

Now opponent is locked near touchline:

```text id="p2"
LW + ST + LCM press triangle
```

* LW presses ball carrier
* ST blocks inside pass
* LCM closes support midfielder

---

## Step 3: Steal Event

### Most common recovery players:

* LW (interception)
* LCM (second ball)
* LB (aggressive overlap intercept)

---

## Step 4: Immediate Attack (0–2 seconds)

### Option A: Immediate cross

```text id="p3"
LW → ST or far-post RW
```

### Option B: Cutback

```text id="p4"
LW → LCM → shot
```

---

## Step 5: Box Movements

* ST attacks near post
* RW attacks far post
* RCM arrives penalty spot
* DM stays edge of box

---

# Pattern 2: Central Press Trap (Midfield Steal → Through Ball)

---

## Step 1: Press Setup

Opponent tries to play through midfield.

### ST + CM press shape:

```text id="p5"
   ST
LCM   RCM
```

They form a narrowing triangle.

---

## Step 2: Forcing Error

Opponent midfielder receives under pressure.

Options reduced:

* Pass back (blocked by ST shadow)
* Pass sideways (intercepted by CM)

---

## Step 3: Steal

Most common winners:

* RCM intercept
* DM steps in
* ST blocks and deflects ball

---

## Step 4: Immediate Vertical Attack

```text id="p6"
RCM → ST
```

or

```text id="p7"
RCM → LW/RW run
```

---

## Step 5: Finishing Movements

* ST runs behind CB line
* Wingers sprint into channels
* CM arrives for rebound shot

---

# Pattern 3: Backline Press (Goalkeeper + CB Error Exploitation)

---

## Step 1: High Press Activation

Opponent CB receives ball.

### ST movement

* Curved run to block GK pass option

### LW/RW movement

* Close fullback outlets

---

## Step 2: Pressing Structure

```text id="p8"
LW  ST  RW
```

Three forwards compress back line.

---

## Step 3: Forced Error

CB options:

* Long ball → intercepted
* Short pass → pressed
* GK pass → blocked

---

## Step 4: Ball Win

* ST wins interception
* LW anticipates pass
* RW pressures weak-side CB

---

## Step 5: Immediate Finish

* Shot within 2 passes
* Cross into box
* Through ball behind line

---

# Pattern 4: Midfield Press Collapse → Quick Combination Goal

---

## Step 1: Press Structure

```text id="p9"
LCM + RCM + DM press midfield triangle
```

---

## Step 2: Ball Recovery

DM or CM wins ball centrally.

---

## Step 3: Instant Combination

```text id="p10"
DM → LCM → ST
```

or

```text id="p11"
RCM → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Movement Synchronization

* ST immediately runs behind defense
* Wingers attack diagonals
* CM joins box

---

## Step 5: Shot Phase

Occurs within 3–4 seconds of recovery.

---

# Pattern 5: Counter-Press to Goal (Gegenpress Attack)

---

## Step 1: Loss of Ball

Team loses possession in attack.

---

## Step 2: Immediate Reaction (0–3 seconds)

All nearby players converge:

```text id="p12"
3–5 players swarm ball
```

---

## Step 3: Ball Recovery

Most common winners:

* nearest CM
* pressing winger
* DM stepping forward

---

## Step 4: Instant Vertical Attack

```text id="p13"
CM → ST
```

or

```text id="p14"
CM → RW/LW run
```

---

## Step 5: Chaos Finishing Phase

* Defense is disorganized
* GK exposed
* Shots taken quickly

---

# Pattern 6: Touchline Press → Cutback Goal

---

## Step 1: Press Trap

Ball forced to sideline.

```text id="p15"
LW + LB + LCM trap zone
```

---

## Step 2: Ball Win

LW or LB intercepts.

---

## Step 3: Immediate Penetration

```text id="p16"
LW → LB overlap or LCM support
```

---

## Step 4: Cutback Zone Attack

### Key runners:

* ST → near post
* CM → penalty spot
* RW → far post

---

## Step 5: Finish

Cutback = highest probability goal chance in football.

---

# Player Roles in High Press Attacking

---

## Striker (ST)

* Primary pressing trigger
* Blocks passing lanes to midfield
* Forces CB errors
* First attacker after steal

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* Trap wide players
* Sprint into channels after recovery
* Attack cutbacks or far post

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Win second balls
* Immediate vertical passing
* Arrive late for shots

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Covers behind press
* Steps forward to intercept
* Starts counter-attacks after steal

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* Join pressing trap
* Intercept wide passes
* Provide immediate overlap after recovery

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Hold high line
* Compress space
* Step into midfield when needed

---

# Core Principle of High Press Attacking

High press attacking follows a strict chain:

```text id="p17"
PRESS → FORCE ERROR → WIN BALL → IMMEDIATE VERTICAL ATTACK → SHOT
```

Key features:

* No buildup phase after recovery
* Maximum 1–3 passes before shot
* Entire team already positioned for attack BEFORE the steal
