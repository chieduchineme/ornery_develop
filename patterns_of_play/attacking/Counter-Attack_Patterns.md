Attack Form 1: **Counter-Attack** is one of the most detailed attacking phases because every player's movement must be synchronized within seconds. I'll assume a common **4-3-3 formation** and break down the movements of all 11 players during the main counter-attacking patterns.

---

# Pattern 1: Vertical Break Counter

### Situation

Your team wins the ball in its own half while the opponent is attacking.

## Initial Positions

```
      ST
 LW         RW

    CM    CM
       DM

LB CB CB RB

      GK
```

---

## Goalkeeper (GK)

### Before Ball Recovery

* Stays high enough to sweep long balls.
* Scans positions of attackers.

### After Recovery

* Immediately looks for:

  * Long throw
  * Long kick
  * Quick pass to center-back

### Objective

Start attack within 1–3 seconds.

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

### Ball-Side CB

* Wins tackle/interception.
* First look is forward, not sideways.

Options:

1. Pass directly to striker.
2. Pass to midfielder facing forward.
3. Long diagonal.

### Far-Side CB

* Spreads wide.
* Provides safety option.
* Covers if attack fails.

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

### Recovery Moment

Moves toward ball.

### Actions

* Receives if CB cannot play forward.
* Turns immediately.

Looks for:

* Striker feet
* Winger run
* Through ball

### Movement

After pass:

* Supports behind play.
* Protects against counter-counterattack.

---

## Left Back (LB)

### If Ball Won On Left

Immediately sprints forward.

Choices:

* Overlap winger.
* Become passing option.
* Occupy wide lane.

### If Ball Won Opposite Side

Runs halfway forward only.

Maintains balance.

---

## Right Back (RB)

Mirror image of LB.

Usually:

* One fullback attacks.
* One remains conservative.

---

## Central Midfielder #8

### First Action

Explosive forward run.

Target:
Space between opponent midfield and defense.

### Role

Becomes:

* Third-man runner.
* Secondary striker.
* Cutback receiver.

---

## Central Midfielder #10

### Recovery Moment

Checks shoulder.

Looks for:

* Open pockets.
* Space behind opponent midfield.

### Movement

Drops slightly.

Receives facing goal.

Then:

* Through ball.
* Diagonal pass.
* Carry ball forward.

---

## Left Winger (LW)

### Immediate Reaction

Sprint behind opponent fullback.

### Run Types

#### Outside Run

```
Goal
 |
 |
 LW -->
```

Attacks channel.

#### Inside Run

```
LW \
    \
     Goal
```

Moves toward center.

### Objective

Stretch defense.

---

## Right Winger (RW)

### Immediate Sprint

Attacks opposite channel.

Purpose:
Force back line to spread.

Creates:

* Passing lane
* Space for striker

---

## Striker (ST)

### First Movement

Checks where center-backs are.

Options:

### Pin

Stays high.

Forces defenders backward.

### Check

Drops toward ball.

Receives and lays off.

### Run Behind

Times run beyond defensive line.

---

## Full Sequence Example

### Second 0

CB intercepts.

### Second 1

DM moves into support.

LW and RW sprint.

ST pins defenders.

### Second 2

CB passes DM.

### Second 3

DM passes #10.

### Second 4

#10 turns.

LW makes inside run.

ST runs channel.

### Second 5

Through ball.

### Second 6

Shot.

Entire attack:
6–8 seconds.

# Pattern 2: Wide Counter

Ball is won centrally but attack goes wide.

---

## Striker

Runs toward near post.

Occupies center-backs.

---

## Ball-Side Winger

Receives ball.

Drives forward.

Options:

* Cross
* Cut inside
* Shoot

---

## Opposite Winger

Sprints far post.

Critical movement.

Many goals come here.

---

## Near-Side Midfielder

Supports underneath.

Creates triangle.

---

## Far-Side Midfielder

Runs into box late.

Arrives unmarked.

---

## Fullback

Overlaps outside.

Provides:

* Width
* Crossing option

---

### Typical Finish

```
Cross

RW  ---> Far Post

ST ---> Near Post

CM ---> Penalty Spot
```

Three runners attack different zones.

---

# Pattern 3: Three-Man Counter

Classic elite-team counter.

---

## Player A (Ball Carrier)

Usually:

* DM
* CM
* CB

Dribbles aggressively.

Draws defenders.

---

## Player B (Decoy Runner)

Usually striker.

Runs opposite side.

Pulls center-back away.

---

## Player C (Scoring Runner)

Usually winger.

Attacks newly created space.

---

### Example

```
     ST ---->

CM ---->

           LW ---->
```

Defense follows ST.

LW receives through ball.

Scores.

---

# Pattern 4: Target-Man Counter

Used by physically strong teams.

---

## Center-Back

Wins ball.

Immediately plays long.

---

## Target Striker

Back to goal.

Objectives:

1. Win header.
2. Shield ball.
3. Lay off pass.

---

## Wingers

Sprint beyond striker.

Don't wait.

Run before striker controls ball.

---

## Midfielders

Attack second ball zone.

Critical role.

Most loose balls land here.

---

### Sequence

```
CB
 |
 |
Long Ball
 |
ST

LW --->

RW --->

CM --->
```

Striker flicks ball.

Runner receives.

Attack continues.

---

# Defensive Security During Counter

Even while attacking:

### Usually Remain Behind Ball

* GK
* 2 CBs
* DM

Structure:

```
      Attack

LW ST RW

  CM CM

     DM

LB CB CB RB
```

This is called **rest defense**.

These players prepare for the possibility that possession is lost.
