# Attack Form: Combination Play (4-3-3)

Combination play is about **rapid, short-pass interactions between multiple players to break defensive structure without relying on long passes or individual dribbling**.

It’s built on:

* 1–2 touches maximum
* tight spacing
* synchronized movement
* third-man runs
* constant triangles

I’ll break it into key patterns and show **exact movements of all 11 players step-by-step**.

---

# Pattern 1: One-Two (Wall Pass Combination)

## Situation

Ball is in midfield or half-space under pressure.

```text id="cp1"
      ST
LW          RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
```

---

## Step 1: Ball Carrier Receives Under Pressure

### Example: LCM

```text id="cp2"
DM → LCM
```

### Movement

* LCM checks into pocket between lines.
* Receives facing forward.

---

## Step 2: Wall Player Appears

### ST or winger drops slightly

```text id="cp3"
LCM → ST → LCM
```

### ST movement

* Drops 3–5 meters
* Attracts CB or DM

---

## Step 3: One-Two Execution

* LCM passes to ST
* ST returns first touch

### Movement synchronization

* ST immediately spins after pass
* LCM bursts forward into space

---

## Step 4: Penetration

LCM now runs into:

* Half-space gap
* Top of box

---

## Final Structure

```text id="cp4"
     LW ST RW
       LCM
```

---

# Pattern 2: Third-Man Combination

---

## Core Idea

Player A → Player B → Player C (who receives free)

---

## Step 1: Build Triangle

Example:

```text id="cp5"
DM → RCM → RW
```

---

## Step 2: Movement Roles

### DM

* Passes and stays

### RCM

* One-touch redirect
* Does NOT turn

### RW

* Times run behind fullback

---

## Step 3: Defensive Disruption

Defender follows RCM → RW becomes free

---

## Step 4: Final Action

* RW receives in space
* Cross or cut inside

---

# Pattern 3: Triangle Rotation Play

---

## Structure

Three players form triangle:

```text id="cp6"
LB — LCM — LW
```

---

## Step 1: Ball Movement Cycle

```text id="cp7"
LB → LCM → LW → LB
```

---

## Step 2: Continuous Rotation

### LB

* Overlaps or underlaps constantly

### LCM

* Moves inside/outside pocket

### LW

* Stays wide then cuts inside

---

## Step 3: Defensive Effect

* Opponent cannot track all movements
* Creates free man constantly

---

# Pattern 4: Diamond Combination (Midfield Core)

---

## Structure

```text id="cp8"
      ST
   LCM   RCM
      DM
```

---

## Step 1: Ball Entry

```text id="cp9"
CB → DM
```

---

## Step 2: Diamond Movement

* DM passes forward
* LCM drops
* RCM pushes
* ST checks

---

## Step 3: Rapid Exchanges

```text id="cp10"
DM → LCM → RCM → ST
```

All 1–2 touches.

---

## Step 4: Penetration

* ST receives between CBs
* Or CM runs beyond

---

# Pattern 5: Blindside Combination

---

## Concept

Attack defender’s "blind shoulder" (area they cannot see).

---

## Step 1: Positioning

Example right side:

```text id="cp11"
RW   RB   RCM
```

---

## Step 2: Movement

### RW

* Hides behind fullback line

### RCM

* Carries ball toward defender

### RB

* Makes overlapping run

---

## Step 3: Combination

```text id="cp12"
RCM → RB → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Result

RW receives behind defense.

---

# Pattern 8: Combination to Third-Man Break

---

## Step 1: Setup

```text id="cp18"
DM → LCM → ST
```

---

## Step 2: Movement

* DM stays
* LCM lays off instantly
* ST runs beyond defense

---

## Step 3: Key Timing

* ST does NOT wait for ball
* Run starts before pass is made

---

## Step 4: Result

* Through ball behind defense
* Or shot from central zone

# Player Roles in Combination Play

## Striker (ST)

* Wall passes
* Drops into midfield
* Runs beyond defense
* Links play

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Core of combinations
* One-touch passing
* Third-man runners
* Rhythm control

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Starts combinations
* Maintains structure
* Finds first forward pass

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* Create triangles wide
* Invert or stretch defense
* Combine with fullbacks

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* Overlaps and underlaps
* Provide width in combinations
* Create overload triangles

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Initiate build-up combinations
* Break first line with passes

---

# Core Principle of Combination Play

Combination play is not about individual brilliance—it is about:

```text id="cp19"
PASS → MOVE → REPLACE → PENETRATE
```

Every pass triggers movement from 2–3 other players.

The goal is to:

* Pull defenders out of shape
* Create a free man
* Exploit the newly opened space instantly
