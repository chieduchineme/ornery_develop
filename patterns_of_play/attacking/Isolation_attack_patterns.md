# Attack Form: Isolation Attack (4-3-3)

Isolation attack is a deliberate method of creating a **controlled 1v1 situation for a high-value attacker (usually not a traditional winger)** in a dangerous zone—typically:

* half-space
* top of the box
* inside channel
* edge of penalty area

Core idea:

> Remove support, stretch the structure, and engineer a “clean duel” for a decisive attacker with space, time, and advantage.

Unlike wing isolation (which is natural wide 1v1), this is about:

* isolating a **central or inverted attacker**
* against a defender who is forced to defend without cover

---

# BASE STRUCTURE (REFERENCE SHAPE)

```text id="ia0"
          ST
LW                    RW

     LCM     RCM
          DM

LB   LCB     RCB     RB
```

Isolation attack usually targets:

* ST (false 9 or hybrid striker)
* LCM / RCM (advanced playmaker)
* inverted winger in half-space (not touchline winger)

---

# PATTERN 1: CENTRAL ISOLATION (STRIKER 1v1 vs CB)

---

## Step 1: Structure Setup

Team compresses midfield:

```text id="ia1"
LCM + RCM + ST in central corridor
```

Wingers stay wide and high.

---

## Step 2: Striker Drops OR Holds Line

Two variations:

### Option A: Drop Isolation

```text id="ia2"
ST ↓ into pocket
```

### Option B: High Isolation

```text id="ia3"
ST stays high between CBs
```

---

## Step 3: Midfield Separation

### LCM / RCM actions:

* one drops to receive
* one pushes forward
* one stays for balance (DM support)

This creates vertical spacing.

---

## Step 4: Isolation Trigger Pass

```text id="ia4"
LCM → ST
```

OR

```text id="ia5"
DM → ST
```

---

## Step 5: Isolation Moment

### ST now isolated:

* 1v1 vs CB
* or 1v2 with split CBs

Defenders are blocked from support due to spacing.

---

## Step 6: Supporting Movements (key disruption layer)

### LW / RW:

* stay wide → pin fullbacks
* prevent CB support collapse

### CM:

* arrive late for rebound

---

## Step 7: Outcome Options

* ST turns and shoots
* ST dribbles past CB
* ST lays off to late runner

---

# PATTERN 2: HALF-SPACE ISOLATION (LCM / RCM ATTACKER 1v1)

---

## Step 1: Structure Creation

```text id="ia6"
LW  LCM  ST  RCM  RW
```

LCM or RCM steps into half-space pocket.

---

## Step 2: Wide Stretching

### LW / RW actions:

* stay wide on touchline
* stretch fullbacks outward

---

## Step 3: Isolation Pass

```text id="ia7"
DM → LCM (half-space)
```

---

## Step 4: Isolation Condition

LCM now faces:

* one CB stepping out OR
* isolated midfielder tracking late

No immediate cover due to stretched defensive line.

---

## Step 5: Movement of Others

### ST:

* pins CBs centrally

### opposite CM:

* attacks box late

### fullbacks:

* hold width or underlap

---

## Step 6: Final Action

* LCM dribbles inside
* shoots from edge of box
* plays through ball into ST or winger

---

# PATTERN 3: INVERTED WINGER ISOLATION (INSIDE 1v1)

---

## Step 1: Winger Inversion

```text id="ia8"
LW → half-space / central channel
```

---

## Step 2: Fullback Positioning

### LB:

* overlaps or stays wide
* pulls defender outward

---

## Step 3: Isolation Creation

```text id="ia9"
LW receives between fullback and CB
```

Now LW is NOT wide anymore—he is inside.

---

## Step 4: Defensive Separation

* CB cannot step out (ST is central threat)
* FB is pulled wide
* CM is too far to help

---

## Step 5: Outcome

LW is isolated in:

* central 1v1
* half-space shooting lane
* through ball channel

---

# PATTERN 4: THIRD-MAN ISOLATION (CREATED DUEL FOR FINAL PLAYER)

---

## Step 1: Triangle Setup

```text id="ia10"
DM → LCM → RW run
```

---

## Step 2: Movement Chain

### DM:

* initiates pass

### LCM:

* bounce pass immediately

### RW:

* blindside diagonal run

---

## Step 3: Isolation Creation

RW receives ball:

* behind defense
* no immediate support defender

---

## Step 4: Structural Effect

* defenders are fixed on ball circulation
* RW arrives “untracked”

---

## Step 5: Outcome

* 1v1 vs CB or GK
* shot or cutback

---

# PATTERN 5: FORCED ISOLATION (DEFENSIVE MANIPULATION METHOD)

---

## Step 1: Overload One Side

```text id="ia11"
LW + LB + LCM
```

---

## Step 2: Defensive Collapse

Opponent shifts heavily toward overload.

---

## Step 3: Switch Trigger

```text id="ia12"
DM → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Isolation Moment

RW now has:

* 1v1 vs fullback
* or open lane into half-space

No immediate support due to defensive shift.

---

## Step 5: Supporting Runs

* ST attacks box
* opposite winger far-post run
* CM late arrival

---

# PATTERN 6: BOX ISOLATION (PENALTY AREA DUEL CREATION)

---

## Step 1: Entry Phase

Ball enters final third.

---

## Step 2: Targeted Player Isolation

Example:

```text id="ia13"
ST isolated vs CB inside box
```

OR

```text id="ia14"
RCM arrives late vs single marker
```

---

## Step 3: Structural Separation

* defenders occupied by runners
* only one defender left for attacker

---

## Step 4: Final Ball

* low cutback
* chipped pass
* through ball

---

## Step 5: Finish

* immediate shot (1–2 touches max)

---

# PLAYER ROLE SUMMARY (ISOLATION ATTACK)

---

## Striker (ST)

* primary isolation target in central zones
* holds or drops to create 1v1
* finishes or lays off under pressure

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* stretch defensive line to prevent help
* create isolation space inside
* also become isolation targets after inversion

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* key isolation creators via passing
* arrive late into isolated zones
* exploit secondary space after isolation is created

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* isolation trigger passer
* switches play to weak-side isolations
* maintains structure during aggressive positioning

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* stretch opposition horizontally
* isolate inverted attackers by dragging defenders wide
* sometimes become secondary isolation runners

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* often forced into 1v1 defensive situations
* step out or hold line depending on isolation trigger

---

# CORE PRINCIPLE OF ISOLATION ATTACK

Isolation attack follows a precise logic:

```text id="ia15"
SPACE MANIPULATION → DEFENSIVE DISTORTION → TARGETED 1v1 CREATION → EXPLOITATION
```

Key ideas:

* isolation is engineered, not accidental
* defenders are “removed” by positioning, not dribbling
* attackers receive advantage BEFORE receiving the ball
* support players exist mainly to REMOVE defensive help
