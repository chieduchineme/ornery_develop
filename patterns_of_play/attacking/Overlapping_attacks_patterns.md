# Attack Form: Overlapping Attacks (Advanced Version – “Overlap 2.0”)

Overlapping attacks are no longer just “fullback runs around winger.” In modern football, they are **multi-layered coordinated runs involving fullbacks, midfielders, wingers, and even inverted strikers**.

The core idea is:

> Create a wide overload → fix defender → release overlapping runner at the exact timing window

I’ll break this into **advanced overlapping patterns** and show **movement of all 11 players step-by-step**.

---

# Pattern 1: Classic Overlap + Timing Fix (Base Structure)

## Situation

Ball on wing in structured possession.

```text id="ov1"
      ST
LW           RW

   LCM   RCM
       DM

LB  LCB RCB  RB
```

Assume left side attack.

---

## Step 1: Winger Receives and “Fixes” Defender

### LW actions

* Receives facing touchline
* Dribbles slowly (key timing action)
* Forces fullback to engage 1v1

```text id="ov2"
LW ↔ LB opponent (fixed duel)
```

---

## Step 2: Overlap Trigger

### LB movement

* Starts from deep
* Curved sprint outside winger
* Times run AFTER defender commits

```text id="ov3"
LB → outside LW → forward channel
```

---

## Step 3: Midfield Support Structure

### LCM

* Moves into half-space pocket (between LB and LW lane)
* Ready for cutback or recycle

### DM

* Holds central safety position
* Prevents counterattack

### RCM

* Arrives top of box late

---

## Step 4: Striker Movement

### ST

* Near-post run OR central drag
* Pulls CB toward ball side

---

## Step 5: Final Decision

### LW options:

* Pass LB for overlap cross
* Cut inside and shoot
* Layoff to LCM

---

## End Structure

```text id="ov4"
LB → cross zone
LW → 1v1 dribble
LCM → cutback zone
ST → near post
```

# Player Role Summary (Overlap Attack 2.0)

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* Fix defenders in place
* Delay or accelerate play
* Decide between dribble, pass, or cut inside
* Trigger overlaps

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* Primary overlapping runners
* Timing-based attackers
* Provide width or inside runs depending on system
* Deliver crosses or cutbacks

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* Create inside support triangles
* Provide underlaps
* Late arrivals into box for second phase

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* Balance structure during overlap
* Prevent counterattacks
* Switch play when overload collapses

---

## Striker (ST)

* Pins center-backs
* Creates space in box for overlap finish
* Attacks near-post zones

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* Initiate diagonal switches
* Support buildup before overlap begins

# Core Principle of Overlapping Attacks 2.0

Overlapping is not just running wide:

```text id="ov17"
FIX DEFENDER → DELAY MOMENT → TRIGGER RUN → EXPLOIT SPACE BEHIND
```

The real weapon is **timing**, not speed.

Key idea:

* Winger freezes defender
* Overlap arrives unseen
* Ball is released exactly when defender is wrong-footed
