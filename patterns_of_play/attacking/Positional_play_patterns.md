# Attack Form: Positional Play (Juego de Posición) — 4-3-3 Structure

Positional Play (often associated with elite Spanish and modern European teams) is not just “a style of attack”—it is a **spatial occupation system**.

Core idea:

> The pitch is divided into zones, and players must occupy specific zones to create **numerical superiority, positional superiority, and qualitative superiority**.

Everything is built on:

* fixed structure (resting positions)
* rotations (to break marking)
* third-man combinations
* constant spacing (width + depth + height)
* attracting pressure in one zone to free another

I’ll break it into **key positional play patterns** and describe **every player’s movement and role step-by-step**.

---

# BASE STRUCTURE (REFERENCE SHAPE)

```text id="pp0"
          ST
LW                    RW

     LCM     RCM
          DM

LB   LCB     RCB     RB
```

But this shape is only the *starting map*. In play, it constantly morphs.

---

# PRINCIPLE 1: REST DEFENSE + STRUCTURE BALANCE

Before attack patterns even begin, positional play always maintains:

## Step 1: Rest Structure

```text id="pp1"
LB  LCB  RCB  RB
        DM
```

### DM

* anchors midfield
* protects against transitions

### CBs

* split wide
* allow circulation

### Fullbacks

* one may advance, one balances

---

## Step 2: Occupying Vertical Lines

The pitch is divided into 5 vertical lanes:

```text id="pp2"
LW | LHS | CENTRE | RHS | RW
```

Each lane must be occupied at all times.

---

# PATTERN 1: 5-LANE OCCUPATION ATTACK (STRUCTURAL POSSESSION BUILD)

---

## Step 1: Initial Occupation

```text id="pp3"
LW - LCM - ST - RCM - RW
```

### Key roles:

* LW + RW = width holders
* ST = central reference
* CM = half-space controllers

---

## Step 2: Fullback Integration

### LB moves into midfield or wing

```text id="pp4"
LB → LCM zone or LW lane
```

### RB holds deeper OR overlaps depending on side

---

## Step 3: Midfield Positioning

### DM

* stays central but slightly behind ball
* always provides passing triangle

---

## Step 4: Ball Circulation Pattern

```text id="pp5"
CB → DM → LCM → LW → LB
```

Movement rule:

* every pass triggers a new positional adjustment

---

## Step 5: Outcome

* overload on one lane
* weak side isolated winger becomes free

---

# PATTERN 2: POSITIONAL ROTATION (INTERCHANGING ROLES)

---

## Step 1: Trigger

Ball enters half-space.

---

## Step 2: Rotation Mechanism

Example left side:

```text id="pp6"
LW ↔ LCM ↔ LB
```

### Movement rules:

* LW moves inside (becomes pseudo midfielder)
* LCM moves wide (becomes winger)
* LB moves high (becomes attacker)

---

## Step 3: Structure becomes dynamic

Now shape becomes:

```text id="pp7"
     ST
LCM        LB
   LW (inside)
```

---

## Step 4: Defensive confusion

Opponents cannot assign fixed markers.

---

## Step 5: Penetration

* LB overlaps into final third
* LW becomes central creator
* LCM shoots or plays through ball

---

# PATTERN 3: THIRD-MAN POSSESSION PROGRESSION

(critical inside positional play)

---

## Step 1: Build triangle

```text id="pp8"
DM → LCM → ST
```

---

## Step 2: Movement logic

### DM

* plays forward pass

### LCM

* one-touch bounce

### ST

* drops into midfield pocket

---

## Step 3: Third-man run

```text id="pp9"
RW → diagonal run behind defense
```

He is NOT involved in earlier passes.

---

## Step 4: Outcome

* ST receives pressure
* RW finishes in space

---

# PATTERN 4: HALF-SPACE DOMINATION (KEY POSSESSION WEAPON)

---

## Step 1: Positioning

```text id="pp10"
LW   LCM   ST   RCM   RW
```

Focus: LCM + RCM occupy half-spaces.

---

## Step 2: Movement roles

### LCM

* drops between lines
* receives under pressure

### RCM

* pushes higher into box edge

### ST

* pins CBs centrally

---

## Step 3: Ball progression

```text id="pp11"
DM → LCM → RCM → ST
```

---

## Step 4: Outcome

* central penetration
* cutbacks or through balls

---

# PATTERN 5: POSSESSION TO OVERLOAD TRANSITION

---

## Step 1: Controlled build

Left side possession:

```text id="pp12"
LW + LB + LCM
```

---

## Step 2: Opponent shift

Defense collapses toward ball.

---

## Step 3: Structural switch

```text id="pp13"
DM → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Re-occupation

### RW

* immediately isolated

### RB

* overlaps into space

### ST

* central runs

---

# PATTERN 6: FALSE 9 POSSESSION SYSTEM

---

## Step 1: Striker drops

```text id="pp14"
ST ↓ into midfield
```

---

## Step 2: Midfield expansion

* LCM + RCM push forward
* LW + RW stay wide

---

## Step 3: Structure becomes:

```text id="pp15"
LW  LCM  ST  RCM  RW
```

(ST now midfielder/playmaker)

---

## Step 4: Rotation play

* ST combines in midfield
* creates overload in central zone

---

## Step 5: Penetration

* winger runs behind defense
* midfielders arrive late into box

---

# PATTERN 7: POSITIONAL FINAL THIRD OCCUPATION

---

## Step 1: Entry into attacking zone

Team reaches final third.

---

## Step 2: Fixed occupation model

```text id="pp16"
LW  ST  RW
LCM      RCM
      DM
```

---

## Step 3: Movement rules

### LW/RW

* stay wide OR cut inside depending on space

### ST

* pins CBs

### CM

* occupies penalty spot zones

---

## Step 4: Ball circulation

```text id="pp17"
side → centre → opposite side → final pass
```

---

## Step 5: Final action

* cutback
* through ball
* edge-of-box shot

---

# PLAYER ROLE SUMMARY (POSICIONAL PLAY)

---

## Striker (ST)

* anchors central zone
* drops or pins CBs
* connects midfield and attack
* creates space for others

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* maintain width
* stretch defensive structure
* invert into half-spaces when needed
* finish attacks or assist

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* main controllers of structure
* occupy half-spaces
* facilitate rotations
* arrive late into box

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* positional anchor
* maintains rest defense
* controls tempo and circulation
* prevents counterattacks

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* provide width or inside support
* join midfield structure
* create overloads
* overlap into final third

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* initiate structure
* control spacing
* step into midfield when needed
* switch play

---

# CORE PRINCIPLE OF POSICIONAL PLAY

Positional Play is defined by:

```text id="pp18"
SPACE OCCUPATION → STRUCTURAL SUPERIORITY → BALL PROGRESSION → ROTATION → PENETRATION
```

Key ideas:

* players occupy zones, not just chase ball
* structure is always maintained
* movement is coordinated, not individual
* space is created before the pass is played
