# Attack Form: Combination Crossing Attack (4-3-3)

Combination crossing attack is a **pre-cross manipulation system** where the cross is not the first attacking action, but the final product of:

> short combinations, rotations, third-man runs, and box occupation before delivery.

Unlike direct crossing, this system is about:

* pulling defenders out of shape first
* creating a better crossing angle second
* then delivering a high-quality cross into a pre-loaded box

The key idea:

> “You don’t cross into a box—you build a box first, then deliver into it.”

---

# BASE STRUCTURE (REFERENCE)

```text id="cc0"
          ST
LW                    RW

     LCM     RCM
          DM

LB   LCB     RCB     RB
```

We assume attacking on the right side for clarity (RW + RB side), but the patterns are symmetrical.

---

# PATTERN 1: WIDE TRIANGLE COMBINATION → CROSS

---

## Step 1: Initial Wide Triangle Formation

```text id="cc1"
RW - RB - RCM
```

### Player roles:

* RW = isolator (holds defender)
* RB = runner/overlap option
* RCM = connector (third-man passer)

---

## Step 2: First Combination Phase

```text id="cc2"
RB → RCM
```

### Movements:

* RW stays wide, pins fullback
* RB overlaps or checks short
* RCM drops into half-space pocket

---

## Step 3: Second Combination Phase

```text id="cc3"
RCM → RW (bounce pass)
```

### Effect:

* defender shifts toward RW
* space opens behind fullback

---

## Step 4: Third Action (Cross Trigger)

```text id="cc4"
RW → RB overlap → cross
```

### Timing principle:

* cross is delivered AFTER 2–3 passes
* defense is already shifting laterally

---

## Step 5: Box Occupation

* ST → near post run
* LCM → penalty spot
* LW → far post
* DM → edge of box rebound zone

---

## Result:

Cross is delivered into a **moving, reorganized defense**

---

# PATTERN 2: HALF-SPACE COMBINATION → CROSS

---

## Step 1: Entry into Half-Space

```text id="cc5"
RCM receives between lines
```

---

## Step 2: Combination Structure

```text id="cc6"
RCM ↔ RW ↔ RB
```

### Movements:

* RW drops short
* RB overlaps aggressively
* RCM becomes pivot

---

## Step 3: Third-Man Release

```text id="cc7"
RCM → RB (run behind)
```

---

## Step 4: Cross Execution

* RB reaches byline
* delivers low or lofted cross

---

## Step 5: Box Occupation Logic

* ST attacks near post
* RW arrives late central
* LCM far-side arrival

---

# PATTERN 3: UNDERLAP COMBINATION → CROSS

---

## Step 1: Wide Fixation

```text id="cc8"
RW isolates LB
```

---

## Step 2: Combination Triangle

```text id="cc9"
RW → RCM → RB
```

---

## Step 3: Underlap Trigger

* RB runs INSIDE channel
* RW holds wide position
* RCM connects one-touch

---

## Step 4: Final Cross Phase

RB now in advanced inside position:

* can cross from inside angle
* or cutback to penalty spot

---

## Step 5: Box Movement

* ST near post
* RW far post
* LCM penalty spot

---

# PATTERN 4: ROTATIONAL COMBINATION → CROSS

---

## Step 1: Rotation Begins

```text id="cc10"
RW ↔ RCM ↔ RB
```

### Key idea:

Positions swap continuously.

---

## Step 2: Defensive Confusion Phase

* marker loses assignment
* fullback unsure who to track

---

## Step 3: Final Rotation Exit

```text id="cc11"
RB ends up wide → cross position
```

or

```text id="cc12"
RCM arrives wide → cross position
```

---

## Step 4: Cross Delivery

* after 3–5 passes
* defense is shifted and disorganized

---

# PATTERN 5: CENTRAL COMBINATION → WIDE CROSS RELEASE

---

## Step 1: Central overload

```text id="cc13"
RCM + ST + LCM
```

---

## Step 2: Short passing chain

```text id="cc14"
RCM → ST → LCM
```

---

## Step 3: Switch release

```text id="cc15"
LCM → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Final cross

RW receives in isolated wide space:

* no immediate pressure
* clean crossing angle

---

## Step 5: Box structure

* ST central duel
* opposite winger far post
* CM late arrival

---

# PATTERN 6: BYLINE COMBINATION → CUTBACK CROSS

---

## Step 1: Progression into box

```text id="cc16"
RW dribbles into box
```

---

## Step 2: Combination support arrives

* RB overlaps
* RCM arrives top of box

---

## Step 3: Tight combination

```text id="cc17"
RW ↔ RB ↔ RCM
```

---

## Step 4: Final action

RW reaches byline:

* cutback instead of traditional cross

---

## Step 5: Box attack structure

* ST near post
* LCM penalty spot
* RW far post rebound

---

# PATTERN 7: DOUBLE CROSS BUILD (RECYCLING CROSS SYSTEM)

---

## Step 1: First cross blocked or recycled

```text id="cc18"
RW cross → cleared
```

---

## Step 2: Second wave formation

* RCM collects second ball
* RB resets position
* LW shifts across

---

## Step 3: Rebuild combination

```text id="cc19"
RCM → RB → RW
```

---

## Step 4: Second cross

* defense is now disorganized and deep
* second cross more dangerous than first

---

# PLAYER ROLE SUMMARY (COMBINATION CROSSING)

---

## Striker (ST)

* anchors central zone
* near-post attacker
* creates space during combinations
* finishes crosses

---

## Wingers (LW/RW)

* initiate wide isolation
* drop into combinations
* decide timing of cross
* finish far-post runs

---

## Fullbacks (LB/RB)

* main crossing providers
* overlap and underlap runners
* key participants in triangle combinations

---

## Central Midfielders (LCM/RCM)

* main combiners
* third-man pass creators
* switch play initiators
* late box runners

---

## Defensive Midfielder (DM)

* stabilizer during attacking rotations
* recycle possession
* prevents counterattacks after loss

---

## Center-Backs (CBs)

* start buildup leading to combinations
* switch play to weak-side cross attacks

---

# CORE PRINCIPLE OF COMBINATION CROSSING

Combination crossing is defined by:

```text id="cc20"
MANIPULATE DEFENSE → CREATE ANGLE → OVERLOAD SIDE → DELIVER CROSS INTO PRE-BUILT BOX
```

Key ideas:

* cross is the *final output*, not the starting action
* passes before the cross are what break defensive structure
* box occupation is pre-planned before delivery
* timing beats power or accuracy alone

---

# FINAL SUMMARY

Combination crossing attacks are the **most structured form of wide attacking**, blending:

* positional play (spacing)
* rotational attack (movement)
* third-man runs (breaking lines)
* crossing variations (final delivery)
