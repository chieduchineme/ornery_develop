# Back Five Defending Patterns

## Core Idea

Use five players across the defensive line to protect width and the penalty area.

A back five can be a starting formation or a defensive transformation from a back four when wingers drop.

## Base Structure

```text
LWB  LCB  CB  RCB  RWB
     CM   CM   CM
          ST
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent attacks with wide overloads
* full-backs or wing-backs push high
* team protects a lead
* opponent crosses frequently
* wide center-backs need support against channels

## Player Movements

* wing-back jumps to wide receiver
* outside center-back covers channel
* central center-back protects the box
* far wing-back narrows to defend back post
* midfield line protects cutback and second-ball zones

## Typical Pattern

```text
Opponent attacks right
LWB presses crosser
LCB covers channel
CB protects six-yard zone
RWB tucks to far post
```

Goal: defend the full width of the box without exposing central defenders.

## Strengths

* strong against crosses
* protects wide and central lanes
* useful for game management

## Risks

* can leave midfield outnumbered
* wing-backs may be pinned deep
* difficult to counter if the striker is isolated

## Elite Usage

Used by Antonio Conte teams, Inter Milan, Chelsea under Thomas Tuchel, and many sides that want secure box protection with wing-back coverage.
