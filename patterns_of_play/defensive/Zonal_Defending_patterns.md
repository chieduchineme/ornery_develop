# Zonal Defending Patterns

## Core Idea

Defend the space, not only the opponent.

Zonal defending keeps the team connected by assigning players to zones and passing opponents on when they leave those zones.

## Base Structure

```text
Line 1: pressure and screen
Line 2: central compactness
Line 3: depth and box protection
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent circulates across zones
* rotations attempt to pull defenders out
* ball enters the half-space
* wide player receives near the touchline
* central forward drops between the lines

## Player Movements

* defenders shift as a unit toward the ball
* player nearest the ball applies pressure
* adjacent players provide cover
* far-side players balance compactness and switch protection
* center-backs pass runners across the line

## Typical Pattern

```text
Ball moves left
whole block shifts left
near CM presses
DM covers inside
far CM protects switch lane
```

Goal: maintain shape while slowly reducing the opponent's useful space.

## Strengths

* protects team structure
* resists rotations better than pure man-marking
* easier to preserve compactness

## Risks

* dangerous if pressure on the ball is weak
* communication needed when runners cross zones
* passive zones can become free receiving pockets

## Elite Usage

Used by nearly every elite team in some phase of the game, especially in mid-blocks, low blocks, and set-piece defending.
