# Compactness Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

Shrink the usable pitch by keeping short distances between players and lines.

Compact defending makes central passing lanes crowded and forces the opponent into slow, wide circulation.

## Base Structure

```text
Vertical compactness: short distance from striker to center-backs
Horizontal compactness: narrow distances across each line
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent tries to play through midfield
* ball enters central lane
* team drops after losing first press
* opponent switches into final third
* defending team protects a lead

## Player Movements

* block shifts together toward the ball
* midfield line stays connected to defensive line
* forwards screen central passes
* far-side winger tucks inside
* back line steps or drops together

## Typical Pattern

```text
Ball at RCB
ST screens DM
LM and RM tuck inside
CMs protect central lane
back four hold close behind midfield
```

Goal: force the opponent around the block instead of through it.

## Strengths

* protects central zones
* makes line-breaking passes harder
* reduces gaps for combination play

## Risks

* wide switches can expose far-side space
* deep compactness can invite crosses
* bad spacing between lines creates pockets

## Elite Usage

Atletico Madrid, Inter Milan, and many elite tournament teams use compactness as the foundation for reliable defending.
