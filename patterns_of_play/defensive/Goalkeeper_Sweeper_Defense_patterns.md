# Goalkeeper Sweeper Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

The goalkeeper defends the space behind a high defensive line.

Sweeper-keeper defending lets the team press higher, hold a compact shape, and reduce fear of balls in behind.

## Base Structure

```text
Back line high
GK starts beyond six-yard box
GK ready for through balls and clearances
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent plays direct behind the line
* center-back steps high to press
* through ball is under-hit
* attacker runs from deep
* team holds a high offside line

## Player Movements

* goalkeeper starts high relative to the defensive line
* center-backs hold compact distances
* full-backs recover inside first
* midfield pressures the passer
* goalkeeper clears, claims, or passes first time

## Typical Pattern

```text
Opponent DM chips behind
CB holds line
GK sprints outside box
GK clears before striker arrives
```

Goal: protect depth so the outfield team can defend aggressively.

## Strengths

* supports high pressing
* reduces danger of balls behind
* helps possession teams recover loose passes

## Risks

* poor timing can create open-goal chances
* requires calm technical distribution
* dangerous if goalkeeper misreads bounce or distance

## Elite Usage

Associated with Manuel Neuer, Ederson, Alisson, and other goalkeepers in teams that defend with high lines.
