# Defensive Overload Patterns

## Core Idea

Create numerical superiority around the ball.

Defensive overloads suffocate dangerous areas by placing more defenders than attackers near the immediate threat.

## Base Structure

```text
Ball side: 4 defenders vs 3 attackers
Far side: compact cover and switch protection
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent overloads one flank
* star attacker receives in isolation
* ball enters half-space
* dangerous cross preparation
* central counter threat appears

## Player Movements

* winger tracks back to create 2v1 wide
* central midfielder slides across
* full-back engages attacker
* center-back covers inside channel
* far-side midfielder protects the switch

## Typical Pattern

```text
LW receives vs RB
RM recovers to double-team
RCM blocks half-space
RCB covers channel
DM protects cutback zone
```

Goal: remove the opponent's local advantage and force a low-quality action.

## Strengths

* neutralizes elite dribblers
* protects dangerous zones
* improves second-ball recovery

## Risks

* creates space away from the ball
* can be beaten by fast switches
* needs far-side awareness

## Elite Usage

Used against dangerous wide players, advanced full-backs, and half-space creators. Many elite teams double-team star attackers in decisive zones.
