# Transition Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

React immediately after losing the ball.

Transition defense decides whether the team counter-presses, delays, fouls tactically, or drops into a block.

## Base Decisions

```text
Can win ball immediately? -> counter-press
Can delay carrier? -> slow attack
No pressure on ball? -> recover into shape
```

## Main Triggers

* possession lost in central midfield
* failed dribble in final third
* loose pass after attacking overload
* cross cleared into opponent's runner
* opponent wins second ball

## Player Movements

* nearest player pressures the ball
* nearby players close forward passing lanes
* defenders drop if there is no pressure on the passer
* midfielders sprint back through central lanes
* far-side players recover toward the middle first

## Typical Pattern

```text
RW loses ball
RW presses
RCM blocks central pass
RB delays winger
CBs drop and narrow
```

Goal: prevent the opponent from attacking an open field.

## Strengths

* reduces danger after turnovers
* supports aggressive attacking systems
* gives clear team reaction rules

## Risks

* hesitation creates open passing lanes
* over-pressing can expose the back line
* needs strong communication between midfield and defense

## Elite Usage

Every elite team trains transition defense because most dangerous chances come before the defending team is organized.
