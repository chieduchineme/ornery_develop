# Set-Piece Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

Defend dead-ball situations with prepared assignments and zones.

Set-piece defense includes corners, free kicks, throw-ins, and penalties. Elite teams treat these as tactical phases, not interruptions.

## Main Structures

```text
Zonal: players defend key spaces
Man-marking: players track assigned opponents
Hybrid: key zones plus individual matchups
```

## Main Triggers

* corner kick
* wide free kick
* direct free kick near goal
* long throw-in
* second phase after first clearance

## Player Movements

* strongest aerial defenders protect central zones
* blockers and runners are tracked before delivery
* goalkeeper controls six-yard area when possible
* edge defender protects rebounds
* outlet player stays ready for clearance and counter

## Typical Corner Pattern

```text
2 players zonal near six-yard line
3 players man-mark strongest runners
1 player protects near post
1 player protects edge
1 outlet high
```

Goal: win first contact, defend the second ball, and exit pressure.

## Strengths

* reduces chaos through clear assignments
* protects key aerial zones
* can launch counter-attacks after clearance

## Risks

* screens and blocks can free runners
* second balls are often more dangerous than first contact
* poor goalkeeper-defender communication creates hesitation

## Elite Usage

Top clubs employ specialist set-piece coaches because dead-ball defense can decide league titles and knockout ties.
