# Rest Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

Defend while attacking.

Rest defense is the team's behind-the-ball structure during possession. Its purpose is to stop counters before they become dangerous.

## Base Structure

```text
Attackers occupy final third
Midfielders support circulation
2-3 players secure central rest defense
```

Common shapes:

```text
2-3 rest defense
3-2 rest defense
2-2 with goalkeeper sweeping
```

## Main Triggers

* team attacks with many players ahead of ball
* full-backs push high
* opponent leaves forwards up for counters
* ball is played into risky central zones
* shot or cross creates rebound threat

## Player Movements

* center-backs stay connected and ready to defend depth
* holding midfielder protects central counter lane
* inverted full-back may form midfield cover
* far-side full-back balances attacking width with defensive security
* goalkeeper positions high enough to sweep behind

## Typical Pattern

```text
LW attacks left
LB overlaps
DM holds center
RCB and LCB split slightly
RB tucks inside as third cover player
```

Goal: attack with numbers while keeping enough structure to counter-press or delay the opponent.

## Strengths

* prevents open counters
* supports counter-pressing
* allows sustained territorial pressure

## Risks

* poor distances leave direct lanes to strikers
* aggressive full-backs can expose wide counters
* requires attacking players to respect defensive balance

## Elite Usage

Vital to teams such as Manchester City, Arsenal, Bayern Munich, and Barcelona, where long attacking phases must be protected against transitions.
