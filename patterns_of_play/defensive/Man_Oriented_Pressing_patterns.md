# Man-Oriented Pressing Patterns

## Core Idea

Defenders press by locking onto nearby opponents rather than simply guarding zones.

Man-oriented pressing aims to remove easy passing options and force the ball carrier into a duel.

## Base Structure

```text
ST -> CB
LW -> RB
RW -> LB
CM -> CM
DM -> AM
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent builds short from the goalkeeper
* midfielders receive with limited support
* center-back carries into midfield
* team wants to deny a specific playmaker
* opponent uses predictable passing lanes

## Player Movements

* nearest defender follows his assigned opponent tightly
* striker presses center-back while screening the pivot
* midfielders step aggressively onto receiving players
* full-backs lock onto wingers when the ball goes wide
* spare center-back provides cover behind duels

## Typical Pattern

```text
GK -> RCB
ST presses RCB
LW locks RB
LCM locks DM
LB locks RW
```

Goal: make every short option feel covered and force a long pass or rushed mistake.

## Strengths

* disrupts rhythm
* denies free midfield receivers
* useful against possession teams

## Risks

* dragged out of shape by rotations
* vulnerable if individual duels are lost
* needs cover behind aggressive jumps

## Elite Usage

Used in different forms by Marcelo Bielsa, Atalanta under Gian Piero Gasperini, and many pressing teams that trust physical duels.
