# Touchline Press Patterns

## Core Idea

Use the sideline as an extra defender.

The touchline press forces the opponent wide, removes central exits, and traps the receiver in a narrow channel.

## Base Structure

```text
Touchline
  |
  | FB receives
  | Winger presses outside shoulder
```

## Main Triggers

* full-back receives near the sideline
* winger receives facing backward
* opponent takes a heavy touch wide
* pass played down the line
* long diagonal lands near touchline

## Player Movements

* winger presses from outside to inside
* full-back steps tight to wide attacker
* near midfielder blocks inside pass
* striker blocks return to center-back
* center-back covers channel behind full-back

## Typical Pattern

```text
Ball to LB
RW presses from touchline side
RCM blocks inside pass
RB locks LW
ST blocks LCB
```

Goal: force a throw-in, clearance, backward pass, or turnover.

## Strengths

* reduces the opponent's passing angles
* creates clear pressing responsibilities
* effective against teams that build through full-backs

## Risks

* beaten by sharp inside bounce passes
* far-side switch can punish over-shifting
* requires winger and midfielder coordination

## Elite Usage

Common in high presses and mid-blocks, especially against teams that rely on full-backs to progress.
