# Counter-Press Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

Press immediately after losing possession.

The counter-press aims to stop the opponent's transition before it becomes an attack. It is both a defensive safety mechanism and an attacking weapon.

## Base Structure

```text
LW    ST    RW
   LCM  RCM
      DM
```

## Main Triggers

* loose touch after a tackle
* opponent wins the ball facing own goal
* interception near a sideline
* poor clearance into midfield
* immediate numerical superiority around the ball

## Player Movements

* nearest player presses the ball instantly
* second player blocks the forward pass
* third player protects the inside lane
* far-side players narrow to stop the switch
* defenders push up to compress the space

## Typical Pattern

```text
LCM loses ball
LCM presses carrier
LW blocks full-back
DM blocks striker
LB steps in for loose ball
```

Goal: win the ball back within a few seconds or force a backward pass that lets the team reorganize.

## Strengths

* kills counter-attacks early
* recovers possession near goal
* keeps attacking pressure alive

## Risks

* one clean pass can beat several players
* demands excellent spacing during attack
* high physical and mental load

## Elite Usage

Central to teams influenced by Jurgen Klopp and Pep Guardiola. It works best when the attacking structure already places players close enough to press after loss.
