# High Press Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

Defend by attacking the opponent's build-up close to their goal.

The high press tries to turn defending into a chance-creation phase: force a mistake, recover the ball, and attack before the opponent can reorganize.

## Base Structure

```text
        ST
LW             RW
    LCM     RCM
        DM
LB   CB   CB   RB
```

## Main Triggers

* bad first touch by a center-back
* backward pass to goalkeeper
* pass into a full-back facing his own goal
* slow square pass across the back line
* isolated defensive midfielder receiving with back to play

## Player Movements

* striker curves run to block the pass across the center-backs
* winger presses full-back from outside to inside
* near-side midfielder jumps to the pivot
* far-side midfielder narrows to protect central switch
* back line squeezes up to reduce space between units

## Typical Pattern

```text
GK -> CB -> FB
ST blocks return
RW presses FB
RCM jumps inside
RB pushes high
```

Goal: trap the opponent near the sideline and force a rushed pass, clearance, or turnover.

## Strengths

* creates turnovers near goal
* limits opponent's clean build-up
* sets an aggressive psychological tone

## Risks

* space behind the defensive line
* large weak-side switches
* fatigue if pressing distances are too long

## Elite Usage

Used by teams that want territorial control and immediate chance creation, especially sides influenced by Jurgen Klopp, Pep Guardiola, Marcelo Bielsa, and Ralf Rangnick.
