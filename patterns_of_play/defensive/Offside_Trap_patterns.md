# Offside Trap Patterns

## Core Idea

Step the defensive line up together to catch attackers beyond the last defender.

The offside trap needs pressure on the passer, timing from the back line, and goalkeeper coverage behind.

## Base Structure

```text
LB   CB   CB   RB
line steps together
GK sweeps behind
```

## Main Triggers

* attacker starts run too early
* passer has poor body shape
* ball is played backward before through pass
* defensive line has clear communication
* goalkeeper is positioned to cover depth

## Player Movements

* center-back leads the line step
* full-backs step with center-backs
* midfield applies pressure to passer
* goalkeeper holds high starting position
* defenders check shoulder before stepping

## Typical Pattern

```text
Opponent AM receives under pressure
CB calls step
back four move up together
striker runs beyond line
pass is delayed
```

Goal: turn depth runs into offside positions or force the passer backward.

## Strengths

* protects high defensive lines
* compresses the pitch
* discourages early runs behind

## Risks

* mistimed step creates a clean chance
* no pressure on passer makes trap dangerous
* VAR-era timing demands precision

## Elite Usage

Used by high-line teams such as Liverpool under Jurgen Klopp and many possession teams that defend far from goal.
