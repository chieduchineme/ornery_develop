# Low Block Defense Patterns

## Core Idea

Defend deep near the box, protect central space, and make the opponent shoot from low-value areas.

The low block accepts territory but refuses access to the most dangerous zones.

## Base Structure

```text
        ST
LM   CM   CM   RM
LB   CB   CB   RB
```

or

```text
        ST
  CM   CM   CM
LWB CB  CB  CB RWB
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent enters the final third
* cross preparation from wide areas
* pass into the half-space
* dribbler attacks the edge of the box
* late runner appears near the penalty spot

## Player Movements

* back line protects the width of the box
* midfield line stays close to the defensive line
* near winger tracks full-back
* center-backs attack crosses and second balls
* striker screens easy recycling passes

## Typical Pattern

```text
Opponent circulates wide
RM drops to help RB
RCM protects half-space
CB attacks cross
LCM tracks cutback zone
```

Goal: absorb pressure, defend crosses, and clear the second ball.

## Strengths

* protects space behind the defense
* makes central shots difficult
* useful when defending a lead

## Risks

* invites sustained pressure
* clearances can return quickly if the team cannot exit
* requires excellent concentration in the box

## Elite Usage

Used by teams that are comfortable defending the penalty area, especially in knockout ties, late-game protection, or against technically superior opponents.
