# Funnel Defending Patterns

## Core Idea

Guide the opponent toward less dangerous areas.

Funnel defending uses body shape, pressing angles, and team positioning to decide where the opponent is allowed to go.

## Common Funnels

```text
Outside funnel: force play wide
Inside funnel: force play into crowded central trap
Backward funnel: force reset to center-backs or goalkeeper
Weak-foot funnel: force receiver onto weaker side
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent has a dominant dribbler
* team wants to deny central progression
* full-back receives near sideline
* midfielder receives under pressure
* striker presses center-back at an angle

## Player Movements

* presser closes one side of the ball carrier
* cover player protects the dangerous lane
* block shifts toward the chosen destination
* far-side players narrow to control switches
* final defender delays rather than dives in

## Typical Pattern

```text
ST presses RCB from inside
RCB forced wide to RB
LW prepares touchline press
LCM blocks inside pass
```

Goal: make the opponent's next pass predictable.

## Strengths

* gives the team defensive control without immediate tackling
* prepares pressing traps
* useful against dangerous central players

## Risks

* bad angle opens the preferred lane
* requires teammates to recognize the funnel
* over-forcing wide can invite crosses

## Elite Usage

Used in almost every pressing system. The best defensive teams do not only press; they steer.
