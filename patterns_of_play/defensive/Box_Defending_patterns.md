# Box Defending Patterns

## Core Idea

Protect the penalty area first.

Box defending focuses on crosses, cutbacks, rebounds, blocked shots, and tracking runners inside the most dangerous area.

## Base Structure

```text
Near post: first center-back
Central zone: second center-back
Far post: far full-back or wing-back
Cutback zone: midfield runner
Edge: second-ball player
```

## Main Triggers

* opponent prepares cross
* winger reaches byline
* full-back shapes for early delivery
* ball is loose after blocked shot
* striker attacks near post

## Player Movements

* near center-back attacks first ball
* far defender protects back post
* midfielders track cutback runners
* spare player protects edge of box
* goalkeeper claims or punches when possible

## Typical Pattern

```text
Cross from right
LCB attacks near-post zone
RCB marks central striker
LB protects far post
DM tracks cutback
CM guards edge
```

Goal: remove clean shots from central box zones.

## Strengths

* protects highest-value spaces
* clear roles against crosses
* useful in low blocks and late-game defending

## Risks

* repeated clearances can invite pressure
* cutbacks are dangerous if midfield line drops too deep
* poor communication leads to free runners

## Elite Usage

Every elite defensive unit needs box defending. It is especially visible in teams that defend deep, protect leads, or face heavy crossing volume.
