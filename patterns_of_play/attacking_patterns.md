In football (soccer), attacking can take many forms depending on the team's style, formation, and game situation.

### 1. Counter-Attack

* Win the ball and attack quickly before the opposition reorganizes.
* Relies on speed and direct passing.

### 2. Fast Break

* Similar to a counter-attack but often starts immediately after a turnover.
* Few touches, rapid forward movement.

### 3. Possession-Based Attack

* Patient build-up with many passes.
* Aims to create openings by moving the opponent around.

### 4. Direct Attack

* Long passes played forward quickly.
* Focuses on reaching attackers in as few passes as possible.

### 5. Wing Play

* Attacks developed through wide areas.
* Uses wingers and overlapping full-backs to deliver crosses.

### 6. Central Attack

* Penetrates through the middle of the pitch.
* Uses through balls, combinations, and attacking midfielders.

### 7. Crossing Attack

* Getting the ball into the box from wide positions.
* Includes early crosses, deep crosses, and cut-backs.

### 9. Combination Play

* Quick one-twos, wall passes, and triangles.
* Designed to break defensive lines.

### 10. Overload Attack

* Creating numerical superiority in one area.
* Example: 3 attackers vs. 2 defenders on a flank.

### 11. Switch of Play Attack

* Moving the ball quickly from one side to the other.
* Exploits space created by the defense shifting.

### 12. High Press Attack

* Winning possession high up the field and attacking immediately.

### 13. Set-Piece Attack

* Attacking from dead-ball situations:

  * Corners
  * Free kicks
  * Throw-ins
  * Penalties

### 17. Overlapping Attack

* Full-back runs outside the winger to create crossing opportunities.

### 18. Underlapping Attack

* Full-back or midfielder runs inside the winger into central spaces.

### 19. Third-Man Run Attack

* Player A passes to B, then Player C makes the decisive run to receive the next pass.

### 22. Positional Play (Juego de Posición)

* Structured attacking with players occupying specific spaces.
* Associated with teams coached by Pep Guardiola.

### 25. Rotational Attack

* Attackers frequently swap positions to confuse defenders.

### 26. Isolation Attack

* Creating a 1v1 situation for a dangerous non-winger attacker.


### 28. Crossing Variations

* Early cross
* Driven cross
* Lofted cross
* Inswinging cross
* Outswinging cross
* Cut-back cross

### 29. Combination Crossing Attack

* Multiple passes around the box before delivering a cross.

### 30. Total Football Attack

* Fluid movement where players interchange positions constantly.
* Associated with Johan Cruyff.

Most modern teams combine several of these rather than using just one. For example, Manchester City often mixes positional play, overloads, cut-backs, and half-space attacks, while Liverpool FC has been known for counter-pressing and fast transitions.
